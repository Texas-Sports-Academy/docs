## Getting Started & Eligibility

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Is Texas Sports Academy Online a good fit if we want to homeschool?">
    It works a lot like homeschooling — your child learns from home on their own schedule with a school-provided laptop — but it's a **virtual private school**, not a homeschool program. That distinction matters for TEFA: the private-school voucher was up to \$10,474 vs. about \$2,000 for homeschool.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do we have to live in Texas to enroll?">
    Yes. Students must be Texas residents and eligible to attend Texas public schools. Active-duty military families stationed in Texas were also eligible for the voucher. If you're moving to Texas before Fall 2026, you can enroll once you establish residency — reserve a spot anytime at [online.sportsacademy.school/apply](https://online.sportsacademy.school/apply).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My child isn't old enough yet — can we still sign up? Do you offer Pre-K?">
    Texas Sports Academy Online serves **grades 3–12** (roughly ages 8–18). Pre-K and Kindergarten are not offered. If your child isn't there yet, you can still apply at [online.sportsacademy.school/apply](https://online.sportsacademy.school/apply) to get on our list, and we'll reach out when they're eligible.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can an 18- or 19-year-old who hasn't finished high school still enroll?">
    Yes. TEFA accepts students up to age 25 who haven't graduated or earned a GED. The self-paced program lets older students catch up privately on their own laptop, without sitting in a classroom with younger students.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is there an application fee for the online program?">
    No — applying to Texas Sports Academy Online is free at [online.sportsacademy.school/apply](https://online.sportsacademy.school/apply). (A \$100 application fee applies only to the Lake Travis physical campus, a separate program.)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My child is already in K12, Connections Academy, or a similar online school — why switch?">
    Most online schools still require 5–6 hours a day. Our personalized software targets about **2 focused hours**, and tuition also includes sports training at a partner facility, a MacBook, and 1:1 tutoring — benefits most free charter programs don't include. K12 and Connections remain free options if you're happy with them.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I need to talk to my spouse first — what should we review together?">
    Totally understandable. Browse [online.sportsacademy.school](https://online.sportsacademy.school) for an overview — tuition is \$10,400/year covering academics, sports training, a laptop, and tutoring, and the [application](https://online.sportsacademy.school/apply) takes a few minutes and isn't a commitment. Helpful videos: [How students learn 2x in 2 hours](https://youtu.be/WJwPpjp09-I) and [Why kids love coming to school](https://youtu.be/twI9GfGpU8A).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can we visit a physical campus before enrolling?">
    Physical campuses don't host shadow days until after mid-August, when the school year begins. In the meantime, join a live Q&A webinar at [sportsacademy.school/home/events](https://sportsacademy.school/home/events). (Physical campuses are separate programs with higher tuition; online students train at local partner facilities.)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Trust & Safety

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Is this a real person or AI? Who is Tessa?">
    You're texting with Tessa, an automated assistant for Texas Sports Academy Online, available 24/7 to answer questions quickly. You can reach the human team anytime (see below).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is this a scam? How do I know it's legitimate?">
    Texas Sports Academy Online is a real, Cognia-accredited virtual private school, part of the Alpha School network (11\+ years, flagship campus in Austin) and using the same academic system. Learn more at [online.sportsacademy.school](https://online.sportsacademy.school).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is my tax return and SSN safe? Does Texas Sports Academy see my financial information?">
    Your tax return and SSN go directly to the state voucher portal (Odyssey) — **not** to Texas Sports Academy. We're the school; we never see your tax documents, SSN, or financial details. The 1040 is used only for income-based voucher priority.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I reach a real person?">
    Join a live Zoom Q&A at [sportsacademy.school/home/events](https://sportsacademy.school/home/events). For dedicated 1:1 time, book a 30-minute call ([English](https://calendly.com/d/cvkj-nyf-8w6/30-minute-call-with-texas-sports-academy-online) · [Spanish](https://calendly.com/brandon-young-texassportsacademy/30min)).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Enrollment & Logistics

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Am I locked in if I apply? Can we change our mind?">
    No. The school application reserves a spot — it isn't enrollment, and it isn't binding. Enrollment finalizes closer to your start date, and you can walk away anytime before then with no penalty or fee. Voucher funds you don't use stay in your ESA.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if we start and it's not working out?">
    You can withdraw anytime — no contract or minimum commitment. Email [online@texassportsacademy.com](mailto:online@texassportsacademy.com), and we'll send a prepaid box to return the MacBook (return shipping covered). Remaining ESA funds can go toward another approved school via Odyssey.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do I transfer my child from their current school?">
    Once you're **accepted** at Texas Sports Academy, give your current school an anticipated last day. They'll reach out to confirm the transfer and we'll handle the paperwork on our end. You don't need to finish every enrollment step first — just be accepted.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I chose the $8,000 Base Plan but don't see it in Odyssey — where is it?">
    The \$8,000 Base Plan (the full academic program without in-person partner-facility training) isn't selectable in Odyssey — our team sets it up manually. Let us know you want the Base Plan and we'll update your account and follow up when it's ready.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Referrals & Perks

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Is there a family referral program?">
    Yes. Refer another family and **both** families earn a free group training session at a facility of your choice. Each family just needs to apply to Texas Sports Academy Online and select a training facility. Grab your referral link from the community at [sportsacademy.school/home/referrals](https://sportsacademy.school/home/referrals).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My gym told me I'd get a free training session — how does that work?">
    When a partner facility refers you and you apply to Texas Sports Academy Online, your child gets one free training session at that facility once we confirm your application. The facility will reach out to schedule it. (This is separate from the family referral program above.)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can my child keep working over the summer?">
    Yes. Enrolled families can continue in Timeback over the summer for **\$500 in June and \$500 in July**. The program is self-paced and available year-round — students keep logging in, completing lessons, and earning XP all summer.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Funding & Tax Help

These answers help families who already applied for the TEFA voucher or are preparing for a future cycle. The 2026–27 application window closed March 31, 2026.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I use voucher money for personal expenses?">
    No. Funds go into an Education Savings Account for **approved education expenses only** — tuition, materials, therapies, assessments. Payments run through Odyssey; you can't withdraw cash or use funds for household bills.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Will the voucher affect my SSI or disability benefits?">
    Benefit interactions vary by situation. Check with your SSI benefits advisor or local Social Security office for a definitive answer for your circumstances — we can't advise on individual benefit cases.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Will the voucher affect SNAP, food stamps, or other benefits?">
    The voucher isn't cash in your bank account — it's deposited into an ESA and pays tuition directly to the school, so you never receive or handle the money, and it isn't earned income. Many families on assistance applied; lower income meant higher voucher priority. For your specific case, confirm with your benefits caseworker.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I'm on Social Security and don't file taxes — do I still need a 1040?">
    Yes. The portal requires a Federal 1040 and won't accept SSA-1099s or benefit statements. Even with Social Security as your only income, you can file a simple return for free (about 15–20 minutes) and list your child as a dependent. Guide: [sportsacademy.school/tax-resources#no-income](https://sportsacademy.school/tax-resources#no-income).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I have zero income — can I still file for the voucher?">
    Yes. You can file a federal return with zero income for free — enter zeros for income and list your child as a dependent. Guide: [sportsacademy.school/tax-resources#no-income](https://sportsacademy.school/tax-resources#no-income).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What's the difference between a W-2 and a 1040? Is a tax transcript the same?">
    A **W-2** is what your employer sends you; a **1040** is your completed tax return — and the portal needs the 1040, not a W-2 or a tax transcript. Download it from the tax software you used, ask your preparer, or file a new return. Guide: [sportsacademy.school/tax-resources#wrong-document](https://sportsacademy.school/tax-resources#wrong-document).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My 1040 won't upload on the portal — what should I try?">
    Upload a **PDF** if possible (from TurboTax, H&R Block, etc.). If photographing, lay pages flat with good lighting; on iPhone, set Camera → Formats → "Most Compatible." Upload only the **first two pages**. Guide: [sportsacademy.school/tax-resources#upload-issues](https://sportsacademy.school/tax-resources#upload-issues).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Someone else claimed my child on their taxes — what are my options?">
    The portal needs a 1040 listing the child as a dependent. You can file for a different year (2024 or 2025) without a conflict, or the person who claimed the child can submit the voucher application using their return.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="My zoned school district isn't showing on the application — what do I enter?">
    Enter the public school district your child is **zoned** for based on your home address (not necessarily their current school). Use TEA's [district finder](https://tea-texas.maps.arcgis.com/apps/webappviewer/index.html?id=51f0c8fa684c4d399d8d182e6edd5d97) — it's for state reporting and doesn't affect eligibility.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I filed taxes in another state — does that matter?">
    No. The portal requires a **Federal** 1040, which is the same regardless of which state you lived in when you filed.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Was the TEFA deadline extended?">
    It was extended once (from March 17 to March 31, 2026) and then closed permanently for 2026–27. There is no further extension or late-application path. The program is expected to reopen for the next school year.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
