Understanding the accreditation status of your child's school matters for college admissions, athletic recruiting, credit transfers, and scholarship eligibility. Texas Sports Academy Online is a virtual private school — not a homeschool program — and that distinction carries real weight when it comes to transcripts, diplomas, and institutional recognition.

<Note>
  In the Cognia accreditation portal, Texas Sports Academy Online is listed under the name **Texas Sports Academy Virtual**. Use this name when searching the portal to verify accreditation status.
</Note>

## Accreditation

Texas Sports Academy Online holds full Cognia accreditation. The physical Texas Sports Academy campus locations and Alpha School are also Cognia accredited.

<Tip>
  To independently verify accreditation, visit the official Cognia accreditation portal and search for **"Texas Sports Academy Virtual"**. Cognia is one of the largest and most respected accreditation bodies in the world, recognized by colleges, universities, and school districts.
</Tip>

## Transcripts, Diplomas, and GPA

Texas Sports Academy Online issues official transcripts that include GPA and class rank — both of which are important for college scholarships and athletic recruiting. Students who complete all graduation requirements receive a private school diploma.

Because Texas Sports Academy Online is an accredited private school, its transcripts carry the same institutional weight as those from any other accredited school.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Is Texas Sports Academy Online a homeschool program?">
    No. Texas Sports Academy Online is a fully accredited virtual private school. This distinction matters: the school issues official transcripts, confers private school diplomas, and is recognized by Cognia. Homeschool programs operate differently and may not carry the same recognition with colleges, districts, or athletic organizations.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Will Texas Sports Academy Online be NCAA accredited?">
    Yes. Texas Sports Academy Online will be NCAA accredited by Fall 2026. Families with students planning to play college athletics at the Division I or Division II level should plan their course selection accordingly and monitor NCAA eligibility center requirements.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can my child participate in UIL sports?">
    No. Students enrolled in Texas Sports Academy Online are not eligible to participate in UIL (University Interscholastic League) sports. Students can compete in club, AAU, travel leagues, and most homeschool leagues — confirm eligibility with your specific league director before registering. The 2-hour academic day is specifically designed to support that kind of training schedule.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does the program satisfy the TEFA annual assessment requirement?">
    Yes. Texas Sports Academy Online uses NWEA MAP testing three times per year, and this is built directly into the regular coursework. This schedule satisfies the TEFA annual assessment requirement. Other approved assessments include STAAR, ACT, SAT, and PSAT.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do AP courses and college credit work?">
    Students can take AP courses through Texas Sports Academy Online. To receive college credit, a student must pass the AP exam with a score of 3, 4, or 5. Each college independently decides whether to accept AP credit and what minimum score they require — AP credit is not automatic. AP exam fees are **not included in tuition** and are paid separately when registering for each exam. Check directly with the colleges your student is considering to understand their specific AP policies.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What does the history curriculum cover?">
    The history curriculum focuses on US Government and core civic principles, including elections, the three branches of government, and how laws are made. The curriculum does not include content that negatively portrays any religious group.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="If my child returns to public school, will their credits transfer?">
    Texas Sports Academy Online maintains complete academic records for every enrolled student. If your child returns to public school, the receiving school district makes the final decisions about which credits transfer and how they are applied. Having full, official transcripts from an accredited institution puts your child in the strongest possible position for that process.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How do official transcripts help with college scholarships and recruiting?">
    Official transcripts from an accredited school — complete with GPA and class rank — are standard requirements for college scholarship applications and athletic recruiting profiles. Because Texas Sports Academy Online is Cognia accredited and issues formal transcripts, your child's academic record meets those requirements in the same way a traditional private school transcript would.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
