Once your enrollment is complete, there are two logins that matter: the **parent dashboard**, where you track your child's progress, and the **student platform**, where your child does their schoolwork. This page walks through both, what to expect in your welcome email, and exactly how your student's first day unfolds.

<Note>
  You'll receive a **welcome email** after enrollment is finalized with your setup steps. If you haven't received it yet, email [team@texassportsacademy.com](mailto:team@texassportsacademy.com) to have it resent — don't worry if your logins aren't active before then.
</Note>

## Parent Dashboard

Your parent dashboard is the Timeback Parent Dashboard at [alpha.timeback.com](https://alpha.timeback.com/).

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the dashboard">
    Open [alpha.timeback.com](https://alpha.timeback.com/) and click **Sign in with Google**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use your own email">
    Sign in with **your own email address** — the one you registered with Texas Sports Academy. This is different from your student's school email. Once signed in, the **Home** view shows your child's progress.
  </Step>
</Steps>

From the dashboard you can see your child's **XP, active time, courses, daily lessons completed, and mastery by grade level** in real time — so you always know exactly how they're doing without waiting for a quarterly conference.

<Tip>
  **Adding a second parent?** Email [team@texassportsacademy.com](mailto:team@texassportsacademy.com) with your spouse's full name, email address, and your children's names, and the team will set up a second linked parent account.
</Tip>

### Understanding "Knowledge Gap Levels"

It is completely normal for the dashboard to show your child working **below their enrollment grade** at first — for example, a 9th grader starting at a 3rd-grade math level. These are **Knowledge Gap Levels**: specific foundational gaps the program fills first, not a measure of your child's overall ability.

Because learning is hierarchical, the system closes foundational gaps with targeted lessons before advancing to harder material. The model is mastery-based — students "test out" of a level by scoring **above 90%** on assessments, and most accelerate quickly once early gaps are filled.

## Student Login

Students log in through the **Alpha Timeback desktop app** — not a web browser. Since Texas Sports Academy provides every student a MacBook, use the Mac App Store link below.

| Device | Download |
| --- | --- |
| Mac (school-issued MacBook) | [Alpha Timeback on the Mac App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/alpha-timeback/id6742113057?mt=12) |
| Windows | [Alpha Timeback on the Microsoft Store](https://apps.microsoft.com/detail/9p4j5bwp49sm?hl=en-US&gl=US) |

Once installed, your student clicks **Log in with Google** and signs in with their school credentials:

- **Username:** `firstname.lastname@2hourlearning.com`
- **Password:** `iloveschool`

<Note>
  The same password — `iloveschool` — also unlocks the school-issued **MacBook** itself. Type it exactly, with no number on the end. If a login still doesn't work, email [team@texassportsacademy.com](mailto:team@texassportsacademy.com).
</Note>

## What the Welcome Email Covers

Your welcome email walks through setup in order:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download the Alpha Timeback app">
    Install the desktop app on your student's computer using the store link for their device (Mac App Store for the school MacBook). Students use the app, not the browser.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log in with student credentials">
    Sign in with **Log in with Google** using `firstname.lastname@2hourlearning.com` and the password `iloveschool`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Take the MAP screeners">
    Your student completes a short screener (about 20–45 minutes each) in **math, science, reading, and language** to find their Knowledge Grade in each subject.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete the placement tests">
    From the **Placement** menu on the left, your student works through each subject's assessment. Together, the screeners and placement tests personalize their learning path.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The email also includes an invitation to the **parent Slack channel** and a link to your **parent dashboard**.

## Your Student's First Day

When your student signs into the Timeback app for the first time, the day follows a clear sequence:

<Steps>
  <Step title="MAP screeners">
    Short screeners (about 30–45 minutes each) in math, science, reading, and language identify your child's **Knowledge Grade** — where they actually have mastery, which can differ from their age grade.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Placement tests">
    Screener results assign your student's first placement tests. From there, coursework automatically adjusts to their level.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Settle into the daily routine">
    Your student begins the daily goal of **120 XP** (about 2 hours of focused work) completed in **25-minute blocks**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The first-day MAP **screener** is used for placement. It's different from the **NWEA MAP growth test** the school runs three times a year to track progress over time.
</Note>
