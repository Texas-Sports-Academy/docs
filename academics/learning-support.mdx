Texas Sports Academy Online's model is personalized and adaptive by design, which makes it a strong fit for many students with learning differences. Every student begins with diagnostic placement, works at their own pace, and advances only after reaching mastery — so instruction always meets a child where they actually are.

<Note>
  **An honest note on fit.** A fully virtual, self-paced program isn't right for every child. Students with significant developmental or intellectual disabilities who need constant in-person support, aides, or hands-on therapy throughout the day may be better served by an in-person setting. We'd rather have an honest conversation about your child's needs than oversell — [join a live webinar](https://sportsacademy.school/home/events) to talk it through.
</Note>

## How the Model Meets Common Accommodation Needs

Many of the supports families rely on are **built into how the program works**:

- **Self-paced = unlimited time.** There's no timed test or class pace to keep up with. Students work until they hit 90% mastery, however long that takes — the kind of accommodation a 504 plan provides is the default here.
- **Individualized by design.** The whole curriculum adapts to each student's level. The purpose behind an IEP — individualized instruction — is baked into the model rather than added on.
- **Assistive technology.** External apps with **speech-to-text and text-to-speech** are available for students who need them; our academics team can help set these up.
- **Human help, not a classroom aide.** There's no classroom, so no aides — instead, students get **1:1 on-demand video tutoring** with real subject specialists when they're stuck.
- **Re-teaching that adapts.** If a student misses a concept, the software re-approaches it in different ways until it clicks; a human tutor is one tap away for anything that's still stuck.

<Note>
  Because Texas Sports Academy Online is a **virtual private school**, public-school IEPs and 504 plans don't carry over as legal documents — but the individualized instruction they're meant to guarantee is built into the program by default.
</Note>

## Support by Learning Difference

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="ADHD">
    The program often works better than traditional school for students with ADHD. Academics are done in **25-minute focused blocks**, not a 6-hour day. The adaptive software keeps students in the **80–85% accuracy** "sweet spot" — challenged but not overwhelmed — and the **Waste Meter** detects scrolling, guessing, or skipping in real time and coaches the student back on track. Once the daily goal is met, the rest of the day is free, which is a powerful motivator for athletes.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Dyslexia">
    Reading instruction (via AlphaRead) generates articles at the student's **specific reading level** and progresses only as they master content — no one is rushing them or holding them back. Because the system is completely individualized, a student works on the right-level material privately, without a classroom seeing their level.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Autism">
    The environment is **predictable, structured, and self-paced** — consistent 25-minute blocks and the same daily goal every day, with no chaotic classroom or unexpected social dynamics during instruction. Adults serve as guides and mentors, 1:1 tutoring is available on demand, and social interaction happens in the more structured, activity-based setting of sports training.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Anxiety & test anxiety">
    No classroom full of peers, no being called on unexpectedly, no racing to keep up. Assessment is **continuous** rather than high-stakes — mastery is shown through daily work, with NWEA MAP three times a year to track growth — so there's no big "test day" pressure. If a student needs to step away during the day, they can, and pick up where they left off.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Processing differences & being behind">
    The system finds a student's actual **knowledge grade** versus their age grade and starts there, filling gaps with no wasted time on material they already know. Students who have always been behind often catch up far faster than in a traditional classroom. "Careless errors" are usually fluency gaps (e.g. times tables not yet automatic) creating cognitive overload — the system identifies and fills those gaps, and the errors fade.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Privacy

We don't probe for details about your child's disability. If you mention it, we acknowledge it and focus on setting the program up to meet your child academically. You're **not required to disclose a disability to enroll** — the program is personalized for every learner regardless.

## TEFA Disability Priority

For the TEFA voucher, a documented disability doesn't guarantee an award, but it does raise a family's **priority tier** (Tier 1 is a child with a disability in a household at or below 500% FPL — selected first).

- **14 qualifying categories:** Autism, Deaf-blindness, Deaf or hard of hearing, Emotional disability, Intellectual disability, Multiple disabilities, Orthopedic impairment, Other health impairment, Specific learning disability, Speech impairment, Traumatic brain injury, Visual impairment, Developmental delay, and Noncategorical early childhood.
- **ADHD** qualifies under _other health impairment_; **dyslexia** under _specific learning disability_; **autism** counts even if diagnosed after age 3; **developmental delay** applies ages 3–9.
- Only **one** qualifying disability — and **one** proof document — is needed.
- **Priority vs. funding:** the Disability Certification Form establishes priority only, while a current **IEP on file with TEA** can raise total funding to **\$30,000** (vs. the standard \$10,474).
