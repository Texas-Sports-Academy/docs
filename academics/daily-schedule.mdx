One of the most practical advantages of Texas Sports Academy Online is that your family controls the daily schedule. There is no mandatory login time, no fixed school bell, and no homework waiting at the end of a long practice. The academic day is built around a single daily goal — 120 XP — which represents roughly 120 minutes of genuinely productive, focused learning. Once that goal is met and mastery is achieved, the rest of the day belongs to your athlete.

## Daily Academic Goal

- **120 XP = ~120 minutes** of productive, engaged learning
- Work is completed in **25-minute Pomodoro-style focused blocks** — concentrated intervals that maximize retention and minimize burnout
- **No homework** — all academic work is finished during the session, not carried into the evening
- Academics can happen **morning, afternoon, or evening** — whatever fits your family's training and travel schedule
- A **parent dashboard** gives you daily visibility into completed lessons and grade-level progress, so you always know exactly where your child stands

## Live Guide Sessions

Live sessions with your child's Guide are held via **Zoom, Monday through Friday**. These are collaborative working sessions — not lectures. A typical session opens with a short group activity in breakout rooms to build connection and set the tone, then students lock into their personalized academic software while their Guide circulates to check in, offer encouragement, and support anyone who is stuck.

<Note>
  **Session punctuality matters.** A session listed as "9–12" means the session **starts at 9:00 AM Central**. Students are expected to join on time at the start of the session — this is not a drop-in window where you can arrive at any point and receive the full experience. Joining late disrupts the group opening and your child's own momentum.
</Note>

### Guide Session Start Times (Central Time)

| Session | Start Time | Approx. End Time |
|---|---|---|
| Morning | 9:00 AM CT | 12:00 PM CT |
| Midday | 12:00 PM CT | 3:00 PM CT |
| Afternoon | 3:00 PM CT | 6:00 PM CT |

Your family chooses **one session time** that works best for your routine and attends that session consistently each school day. You are not locked into the same time slot forever — if your training schedule changes seasonally, you can switch — but consistency within a session group helps build the accountability and community that make the model work.

### When Live Sessions Are Optional

Enrolled students are expected to attend one Guide session per school day. There is one exception: once your child is **3 or more school days ahead of pace** (approximately 360 XP ahead), daily sessions become **optional for as long as they stay ahead**. If they fall behind, they return to daily attendance until they're back on pace — which typically takes only a day or two.

## Your First Week: Getting Started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Orientation and Account Setup">
    Before your first academic day, you'll receive login credentials for all learning platforms and a walkthrough of the parent dashboard. Your Guide will introduce themselves and explain what to expect in your first live session.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Diagnostic Screeners (Day 1)">
    On your first day, your child completes **30–45 minute diagnostic screeners** in math, science, reading, and language. These assessments identify their **Knowledge Grade** in each subject — the precise level where their skills actually are, regardless of their enrollment grade. This is where instruction begins.
  </Step>
  <Step title="First Live Guide Session">
    Your child joins their assigned Guide session (9 AM, noon, or 3 PM CT) and meets their cohort. The Guide walks everyone through the platform, sets expectations, and gets students into their first real learning blocks. It's a low-pressure introduction to the daily rhythm.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Establish Your Daily Routine">
    By the end of week one, your family will have settled into a daily flow that works: academics in the morning or afternoon, sports training on the other side, and evenings free. Most families find that the structure becomes natural within the first week.
  </Step>
  <Step title="First NWEA MAP Benchmark">
    Within the first few weeks, your child will complete their first NWEA MAP assessment — a nationally normed test that establishes a baseline and tracks growth across the year. This gives you an objective external measure of where your child is starting from.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Flexibility for the Traveling Athlete

The self-paced, location-independent design of the program is built with competitive athletes in mind:

- **Tournament travel:** Work ahead before a big tournament so the days you're competing or traveling don't count against your pace
- **Hotel rooms and road trips:** The software runs in any browser — all you need is a device and an internet connection
- **Planned breaks:** Families can schedule their own days off and breaks in advance; just coordinate with your Guide so the plan is on the calendar
- **Makeup days:** If an unexpected travel conflict means a missed day, students can make it up without falling behind pace — the system tracks everything

The goal is a program that fits around your athlete's competitive life, not one that competes with it.
