## How AP Credit Works

Completing an AP course at Texas Sports Academy Online prepares you to sit for the **College Board AP Exam** in that subject. If you score a **3, 4, or 5** on the exam, you may be eligible to receive college credit — but it is important to understand how this works:

- **Each college sets its own policy.** There is no universal rule about which scores a college accepts or how much credit they award. Some schools accept a 3; others require a 4 or 5. Some award credit for certain AP subjects but not others.
- **Credit is not automatic.** You must check the specific AP credit policy of every college you are considering.
- **The program prepares you to pass.** The AP coursework at Texas Sports Academy Online is designed to prepare you to actually succeed on the exam — not just complete the course material.

<Note>
  **AP exam fees are not included in tuition.** The AP coursework (your preparation) is fully covered by your tuition. However, when you register to sit for the official AP Exam through the College Board, you pay the exam fee separately at that time.
</Note>

## Course Completion Time

Each AP course takes approximately **80 hours to complete**. Because your academic day is structured around focused, efficient learning rather than a fixed classroom schedule, you can move through a course at whatever pace your mastery allows. Students who are ahead of pace academically and have available learning time can complete multiple AP courses in the same school year.

## Transcripts, GPA, and Class Rank

Texas Sports Academy Online provides official transcripts, GPA calculations, and class rank for all enrolled students. These records reflect your AP coursework and are an important part of your college application and athletic scholarship packages. Coaches and admissions offices at colleges and universities expect to see official academic records — and yours will accurately represent the rigor of your AP course load.

## NCAA Accreditation

<Note>
  Texas Sports Academy Online will be **NCAA accredited by Fall 2026**. Once accredited, your coursework — including AP courses — will be formally recognized for **NCAA eligibility purposes**. If you are being recruited to play a sport in college, this accreditation ensures that the courses you complete here count toward your NCAA academic eligibility requirements.
</Note>
