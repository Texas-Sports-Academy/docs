Texas Sports Academy Online covers every core academic subject using best-in-class personalized learning apps — each selected because it adapts to your child's demonstrated skill level, not their age or enrollment grade. Rather than one static textbook for an entire class, every student works through a tailored path that challenges them appropriately from day one. Parents don't need to teach: the software handles instruction, grading, and progress tracking entirely, with 1-on-1 human tutoring available on demand via video call whenever a student gets stuck.

## Subject Details by Grade Band

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Math — All Grade Levels">
    **App: Math Academy**

    Math Academy is the most advanced adaptive math platform available for K–12 students. It diagnoses your child's exact mathematical Knowledge Grade on day one and builds a personalized path forward from there, covering every concept in the precise sequence needed to build a solid foundation.

    - **Grades 3–8:** Students begin at their diagnosed Knowledge Grade, which may draw on content from grades 4–8 depending on where they actually are. Advancement requires mastery at each step — no moving on until gaps are closed.
    - **High school:** The curriculum includes dedicated SAT Math preparation, a path to **AP Calculus AB and BC**, and **AP Statistics**. Students who enter high school ahead of pace can complete multiple levels in a single year.
    - Students who are already advanced start at their Knowledge Grade and can typically cover **two grade levels per subject per year**.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Reading — All Grade Levels">
    **App: AlphaRead**

    AlphaRead generates articles precisely calibrated to each student's current reading level, so your child is always reading text that challenges them without overwhelming them. As comprehension improves, article complexity increases automatically.

    - **Grades 3–8:** The target milestone is passing the **Grade 8 Reading Test with 90% mastery**. Until that benchmark is reached, students continue working through leveled reading content to close any comprehension gaps.
    - **High school:** The reading program shifts toward SAT preparation, with a target score of **800 on the English SAT** section.

    **TeachTales** supplements reading at all levels by connecting narrative reading to each student's personal interests. The philosophy: *read what you love until you love to read.* When students are genuinely interested in what they're reading, fluency and comprehension build faster.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Writing — All Grade Levels">
    **App: AlphaWrite**

    AlphaWrite moves students through a structured writing progression — from crafting individual sentences to composing full, multi-paragraph essays — adapting to each student's demonstrated skill level throughout.

    - **Grades 3–8:** Students work toward passing the **Grade 8 Writing Test with 90% mastery**, at which point they advance to an **AP English track**.
    - **High school:** Writing continues through AP-level coursework, including AP English Language & Composition and AP English Literature & Composition preparation.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Vocabulary and Grammar — All Grade Levels">
    **Vocabulary Apps: Membean, Vocabulary G12**

    Membean uses adaptive memory techniques to build a deep, lasting vocabulary rather than short-term memorization. Students progress through levels up to **Level 11**, which covers the full range of SAT vocabulary. Vocabulary G12 extends this work through 12th grade.

    A full suite of vocabulary, grammar, and SAT/ACT preparation tools supports students across grade levels, ensuring that language skills keep pace with reading and writing development.

    **Grammar App: eGUMPP**

    eGUMPP is a complete grammar and usage program delivered in two phases:

    - **Phase 1:** Students must score **7 or higher** on each module and pass the **Grade 8 Language Test** before advancing.
    - **Phase 2:** Students complete the eGUMPP program entirely, covering all grammar and usage concepts through a high-school level.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Science — Grades 3–5">
    **App: MobyMax**

    Elementary students (grades 3–5) build their science foundation through MobyMax, an adaptive platform that covers core life, earth, and physical science concepts. MobyMax adjusts to each student's level and tracks mastery before advancing.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Science — Grades 6–8">
    **App: Khan Academy**

    Middle school science is delivered through Khan Academy's structured subject sequences, covering:

    - **Biology**
    - **Earth & Space Science**
    - **Chemistry**
    - **Physics**

    A **90% mastery score** is required to advance from one unit to the next in each course. Students who enter middle school having already mastered some of this content begin at the appropriate point in the sequence.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Science — Grades 9–12">
    **Apps: Edmentum, AP Science platforms**

    High school science begins with **Edmentum Biology** and progresses into the AP science track. Available AP science courses include AP Biology, AP Chemistry, AP Physics 1, and AP Environmental Science. Students advance to AP courses when they have demonstrated readiness through their mastery scores in prerequisite coursework.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="NIL Life Skills — All Grade Levels">
    **Proprietary Curriculum**

    Every student at Texas Sports Academy Online, regardless of grade level, participates in NIL Life Skills — a curriculum built specifically for student-athletes preparing for public life and the responsibilities that come with it. Topics include:

    - **Public speaking** — practicing press conference scenarios, media interviews, and confident communication under pressure
    - **Financial literacy** — understanding income, budgeting, contracts, and long-term wealth management
    - **Grit and receiving feedback** — developing the mental resilience to hear criticism, stay coachable, and persist through setbacks

    These skills are embedded in the academic day, not treated as an optional add-on.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Testing and Progress Tracking

Texas Sports Academy Online uses multiple layers of assessment to ensure your child is genuinely progressing — not just completing lessons.

- **NWEA MAP:** Administered **three times per year**, MAP is a nationally normed assessment that tracks your child's growth relative to students across the country. It provides an objective, external measure of academic progress.
- **Internal mastery benchmarks:** Daily and unit-level checks use **STAAR-aligned questions** to measure concept mastery. These are for internal mastery verification only — not the STAAR exam itself — and are how the software determines when a student is ready to advance.
- **SAT/ACT preparation:** SAT prep is woven into the curriculum from middle school onward. The standard target is a **1350+ SAT score**; students on the honors track target **1550+**. High school students also have access to a full ACT preparation sequence.

<Note>
  Students who are already performing above grade level begin at their Knowledge Grade — not at the start of their enrollment grade. This means advanced students spend zero time on material they've already mastered, and they can realistically cover **two grade levels per subject in a single school year**.
</Note>
