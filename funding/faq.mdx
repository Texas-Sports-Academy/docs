Whether you're sorting out your TEFA voucher status, comparing payment options, or just trying to understand what tuition actually covers, you'll find straightforward answers below. If your question isn't listed here, reach out to the team at [online@texassportsacademy.com](mailto:online@texassportsacademy.com).

<Note>
  **Already applied for the TEFA voucher?** Check your current status — including award, waitlist position, or next steps — directly in your Odyssey portal: [withodyssey.com/en/texas-esa/dashboard](https://withodyssey.com/en/texas-esa/dashboard). Most Tier 1 and Tier 2 notifications went out in late April / early May 2026.
</Note>

<AccordionGroup>

  <Accordion title="Is this school free with the TEFA voucher?">
    For families who applied before March 31, 2026 and were approved, yes — the $10,474 Education Savings Account (ESA) covers 100% of the $10,400 tuition, with nothing owed out of pocket. The application window is now closed, so new families enroll at the self-pay rate of $10,400/year. The TEFA program is expected to open again in early 2027 for the 2027–2028 school year.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What does tuition include?">
    Tuition covers everything your student needs for the full program:

    - **Academics** via the 2 Hour Learning adaptive software platform
    - **Sports training** at a Texas Sports Academy partner facility
    - **Apple MacBook** for educational use while enrolled
    - **1:1 on-demand tutoring**
    - **NIL life skills courses** (name, image, and likeness preparation)
    - **College recruiting support**
    - **Parent dashboard** for real-time academic visibility

    AP coursework is included; AP exam fees are paid separately to College Board.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is the voucher a loan or a grant?">
    Neither. The TEFA voucher is state funding deposited into an Education Savings Account (ESA) that you own. Tuition is paid directly from the ESA to Texas Sports Academy — you never handle the money, and you never pay it back. It is not a loan, not taxable income, and not a personal cash payment.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Will the voucher affect my government benefits (SSI, SNAP, etc.)?">
    The voucher is not cash paid to you. State funding flows into an ESA and is paid directly to the school as tuition — it does not pass through your hands as personal income or an asset. That said, every family's benefit situation is different. For guidance specific to your circumstances, consult your benefits advisor or caseworker before making any decisions.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is the voucher taxable income?">
    No. The TEFA voucher is not taxable income. The state funding goes into an ESA and pays tuition directly to Texas Sports Academy — you do not receive it as personal income and do not need to report it to the IRS. If you have specific tax questions, consult a qualified tax professional.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I didn't apply for the voucher. Can I still enroll?">
    Yes. Enrollment is open to all families at the self-pay rate of **$10,400/year** — the same full program, same experience, no voucher required. You can apply and start enrollment at [online.sportsacademy.school/apply](https://online.sportsacademy.school/apply). If the TEFA program reopens for the 2027–2028 school year, you'll have the opportunity to apply at that time.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="When will I hear back about my voucher?">
    Most Tier 1 and Tier 2 notifications went out in **late April / early May 2026**. If you haven't received a notification, check your application status in the Odyssey portal under Application History: [withodyssey.com/en/texas-esa/dashboard](https://withodyssey.com/en/texas-esa/dashboard). If you were awarded, you have until **July 15, 2026** to confirm your enrollment and select Texas Sports Academy as your school.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I'm on the waitlist. Is there still a chance?">
    Yes. Being on the waitlist does not mean you won't receive funding. Waitlisted families automatically move up when awarded families opt out or fail to confirm their enrollment by the July 15, 2026 deadline. Log in to your Odyssey portal regularly to monitor your position and watch for email notifications from Odyssey.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I have multiple children. Does each get a voucher?">
    Yes — the TEFA voucher is per student, not per family. Each eligible child receives their own $10,474 ESA. All of your children needed to be included on a single TEFA application submitted before the March 31, 2026 deadline. The application window is now closed for new applicants, but if approved, each child on your application receives their own individual award.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I use the voucher for my child's existing sports club fees?">
    Not directly. The TEFA voucher funds an ESA that pays **tuition** to an approved private school. Sports training at a partner facility is included as part of Texas Sports Academy Online's tuition — so tuition covers your child's training through the school's program. The ESA cannot be used to pay external club fees, third-party facility costs, or sports organizations directly. If your child's club or training facility becomes a Texas Sports Academy partner, that training would be covered as part of the tuition program.
  </Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>
