This page is a reference for the documents and identifiers used in the **TEFA (Texas Education Freedom Account)** application. Use it to verify what your existing application included or to understand a request from the Odyssey portal.

<Warning>
  The TEFA application window **closed on March 31, 2026**. This is reference material for families who already applied. New families can enroll at the self-pay rate at [online.sportsacademy.school/apply](https://online.sportsacademy.school/apply). The program is expected to reopen for the 2027–2028 school year.
</Warning>

## Document Checklist at a Glance

| Requirement | What's accepted |
| --- | --- |
| Residency | Texas DL/State ID (preferred), or utility bill / lease / mortgage / voter registration |
| Income | Federal Form 1040 (2024 or 2025), first two pages, child claimed as dependent |
| Child's lawful presence | Validated automatically via SSN or parent DL; backup documents only if prompted |
| Disability (optional) | IEP on file with TEA, or Disability Certification Form / FIIE / out-of-state or expired IEP |

## Residency Verification

Provide **one** of the following, in the parent/guardian's name and matching the residential address on the application:

- **Preferred:** Texas Driver's License number or Texas State ID number
- **Alternative:** utility bill, lease agreement or mortgage statement, or voter registration certificate

<Note>
  **Active-duty military families** moving to Texas can use current active-duty orders placing the household in Texas, or orders indicating the family will reside in Texas during the 2026–27 school year.
</Note>

## Income Verification

Every applicant must provide a **Federal Form 1040 tax return**:

- Tax year **2024 or 2025** — whichever you filed most recently
- The **first two pages** only, with **Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)** clearly visible
- The child must be claimed as a **dependent** on the return

<Warning>
  A Federal 1040 is required for **all** applicants — there are no alternative documents. If you have zero income and have never filed, you can still file a simple \$0 return to generate a 1040 for free through [IRS Free File](https://www.irs.gov/filing/free-file-do-your-federal-taxes-for-free). It takes about 15–20 minutes.
</Warning>

<Tip>
  **Trouble uploading on the portal?** Trim the file to just the first two pages, try a different image format, or upload a screenshot instead of a PDF. If it still fails, contact Odyssey support at [help.tx@withodyssey.com](mailto:help.tx@withodyssey.com).
</Tip>

## Child's Lawful Presence

Odyssey validates lawful status automatically using the parent's driver's license / Real ID or the child's Social Security number. **If additional documentation is requested,** provide one of: birth certificate, report of birth abroad, certificate of citizenship, certificate of naturalization, or other official documentation of lawful admission.

## Disability Documentation

Disability documentation is optional, but it can affect both your **priority** and your **funding amount**. The distinction is important:

<Note>
  **Priority vs. funding.** A current **IEP on file with TEA** can qualify a child for up to **\$30,000** in funding. The **Disability Certification Form** (and similar documents) establishes **priority only** — it does **not** increase the funding amount.
</Note>

**If your child has an IEP on file with TEA** (2023–24 through the upcoming school year), have ready: the numerical instructional code, the next annual review (ARD) date, the Texas Unique Student ID (UID), S-Number if applicable, the child's SSN, and date of birth. No upload is needed — entering the UID lets the system verify the IEP with TEA automatically.

**If your child does not have an IEP on file with TEA,** upload **one** of: the TEFA Disability Certification Form, a Full Individual and Initial Evaluation (FIIE), an out-of-state IEP, or an expired Texas IEP.

### The Disability Certification Form

If your child has a disability but no IEP on file with TEA, the [TEFA Disability Certification Form](https://educationfreedom.texas.gov/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/TEFA-Disability-Form.pdf) establishes priority for admission. You provide the form; a **licensed professional** completes and signs it. Different disabilities require different professionals:

| Disability | Required professional(s) |
| --- | --- |
| Autism | Any qualified licensed professional |
| Deaf or hard of hearing | Licensed audiologist |
| Deaf-blindness | Speech/language therapist or licensed speech-language pathologist |
| Emotional disability | Any qualified licensed professional |
| Intellectual disability | Any qualified licensed professional |
| Multiple disabilities | Any qualified licensed professional |
| Orthopedic impairment | Licensed physician |
| Other health impairment (includes ADHD) | Licensed physician, physician assistant, or advanced practice registered nurse |
| Specific learning disability (includes dyslexia) | A diagnostician (school psychologist, educational diagnostician, speech-language pathologist, or remedial reading teacher) **and** the child's general education or qualified teacher |
| Speech impairment | Certified speech and hearing therapist or licensed speech-language pathologist |
| Traumatic brain injury | Licensed physician |
| Visual impairment | Licensed ophthalmologist or optometrist, a certified teacher of students with visual impairments, **and** a certified orientation and mobility specialist |
| Noncategorical early childhood | Any qualified licensed professional |
| Developmental delay | Any qualified licensed professional |

When submitting the form, also include the name, office, and phone number of the certifying practitioner. Questions about the form go to [disabilityform.tefa@cpa.texas.gov](mailto:disabilityform.tefa@cpa.texas.gov).

## Finding Your Child's Texas Unique Student ID (UID)

You only need a UID if your child has **attended a Texas public school** or has an **IEP on file with TEA**. If neither applies, you may not need one.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Online lookup (fastest)">
    If your child has attended a Texas public school, use the [TEA Student Look Up tool](https://tealprod.tea.state.tx.us/Tea.Widgets/Student/?qid=21&ref=). Click **Student Look Up** and enter your child's first name, SSN, and date of birth. (This tool does not work for private-school students who only have a district IEP.)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Contact your school district">
    Your public school district (ISD) is the source of truth. If your child has an IEP but attends private school, contact the special education department of the ISD that created the IEP. If your child has only attended private school, your local ISD can enter the IEP into TEA's APEX system to generate a UID.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Placeholder (last resort)">
    If you've exhausted every option, you may enter **"11111" followed by your zip code** (e.g., `1111178701`) to continue. Note: using the placeholder means you will **not** receive special education funding — that requires a validated IEP on file with TEA.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## S-Number vs. UID

These are two different student identifiers:

- **UID** — a Texas Education Agency identifier assigned when a child enrolls in a Texas public/charter school or has a registered IEP on file with TEA. It never begins with `0` and is used to validate priority and funding.
- **S-Number** — begins with `S` followed by 8 digits (e.g., `S12345678`). Assigned to students enrolled in Texas public schools **without** a Social Security number. Optional — only enter it if your child was assigned one.

Most families only need the UID. If your child has never attended Texas public school and has no IEP on file, you may need neither.

## IEP Instructional Codes

If your child has an active Texas IEP on file with TEA, the application asks for a numerical instructional code from a dropdown. Find it in your child's IEP under the **Least Restrictive Environment (LRE)**, **Instructional Arrangement/Setting**, or **Local Variation** section.

| Code | Description |
| --- | --- |
| 00 | No instructional setting (such as Speech Therapy) |
| 01 | Homebound |
| 02 | Hospital class |
| 08 | Vocational Adjustment Class/Program |
| 30 | State Supported Living Centers |
| 40 | Mainstream |
| 41 | Resource Room/Services — Less than 21% |
| 42 | Resource Room/Services — At least 21% and less than 50% |
| 43 | Self-Contained, Regular Campus — At least 50% and no more than 60% |
| 44 | Self-Contained, Regular Campus — More than 60% |
| 45 | Full-Time Early Childhood Special Education Setting (ages 3–5 only) |
| 50 | Residential Nonpublic School Program |
| 60 | Nonpublic Day School |
| 81–89 | Residential Care and Treatment Facility (various settings) |
| 91–98 | Off Home Campus (various settings) |

<Tip>
  If you're unsure which code applies, check your child's most recent IEP or contact the special education department at their school district.
</Tip>

## General Information Checklist

The application also collected: the parent/guardian's SSN or ITIN, Texas DL/State ID number (if available), the child's SSN, the child's S-Number (if applicable), the child's UID (if previously enrolled in public/charter school), and the child's planned educational setting for 2026–27 (**Private School** or Homeschool).

<Note>
  To fund Texas Sports Academy Online's full tuition, the educational setting must be **Private School** — see [the TEFA Voucher page](/funding/tefa-voucher) for why this matters.
</Note>
