Because Texas Sports Academy Online is a virtual program, attendance is measured through **consistent engagement** in academics, training, and life skills — not seat time or a daily bell. This page explains what's expected of enrolled students, how absences work, and how flexibility is earned.

<Note>
  This is the policy for **Texas Sports Academy Online**. Physical Texas Sports Academy campuses follow a separate, seat-time attendance policy with set hours and tardy tracking. The full Attendance & Engagement Policy is included in your [enrollment paperwork](https://sportsacademy.school/home/enrollment/paperwork).
</Note>

## What Counts as Attendance

An enrolled student is considered in attendance when they consistently engage in the core parts of the program each school day:

- Complete **120 XP** of academics through the Timeback platform
- Attend one **Daily Academics Call** (unless ahead of pace — see below)
- Stay on pace with their academic plan
- Participate in **virtual and in-person sports training**
- Participate in **life skills programming** as assigned

Engagement is reviewed through platform activity, XP completion, assignment completion, Daily Academics Call attendance, training logs, and parent/coach communication.

## Daily Academics Calls

Daily Academics Calls run **Monday through Friday** — live working sessions where students do their own personalized academics alongside a Guide and other students for structure and support. Each student attends **one call per school day**, choosing from three start times (Central):

| Call | Start Time | Length |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Morning | 9:00 AM CT | 3 hours |
| Midday | 12:00 PM CT | 3 hours |
| Afternoon | 3:00 PM CT | 3 hours |

The three slots provide flexibility — if a conflict or emergency comes up, your student can attend a different call the same day. For the full daily rhythm, see [Daily Schedule](/academics/daily-schedule).

### Call Conduct

During a Daily Academics Call, students are expected to:

- Join at the **start** of the scheduled session
- Keep their **camera on** unless otherwise approved
- Use the **TimeBack app** for focused academic work
- Follow their Guide's instructions and participate in check-ins
- Avoid distractions — unrelated sites, games, videos, or phones
- Make meaningful progress toward their XP target

<Warning>
  Simply logging in isn't enough. A student may be marked as not meaningfully participating if they join the call but don't stay engaged in academic work.
</Warning>

### Recording

Calls may be **recorded** for educational, safety, attendance, quality-assurance, and program-accountability purposes, and the school announces when a call is being recorded. Signing the Attendance & Engagement Policy consents to participation in recorded calls. Families who don't consent to camera/microphone participation must notify the school **in writing**; an alternative arrangement (such as camera/mic off) may be approved, but the student must remain present, responsive, and engaged. Recordings are stored securely, access-limited, retained up to one school year, and never posted publicly or used for marketing without separate written consent.

## Flexibility When You're Ahead

The policy is supportive, not punitive: **get ahead, earn flexibility; fall behind, get more structure.**

- A student who is **at least 3 school days (about 360 XP) ahead** of pace may **skip the Daily Academics Call** for as long as they stay ahead.
- If they drop below that threshold, the daily call becomes required again until they're back on pace — usually a day or two.

This is what makes the program work around training and travel: working ahead banks flexibility for tournaments, trips, and competition days.

## Excused Absences

Reduced engagement may be excused for:

- Personal illness
- Medical, dental, or other professional appointments (with documentation)
- Serious illness or death in the immediate family
- Emergency circumstances approved by the Academic Director
- Authorized religious holidays
- Approved school-sponsored activities
- **Athletic competitions, showcases, tournaments, and related travel**
- Other circumstances approved in advance by the Academic Director

Students may miss up to **5 school days per semester** with a parent note for otherwise-excused reasons. Beyond that, medical or other official documentation is required, or the absence may be treated as unexcused.

<Tip>
  **Traveling for sports?** Work ahead before the trip, stay engaged when reasonable, and get back on track promptly after returning. The software picks up exactly where your student left off, and XP targets adjust to help them catch up.
</Tip>

## If a Student Falls Behind

Excessive absences, low engagement, or not completing the required 120 XP per school day may lead to additional support and, eventually, further action:

- Additional monitoring and required check-ins
- Required Daily Academics Call attendance
- **Paused payments toward facility training and coaching** until the student is back on pace
- Withdrawal from the program if a student continues to fall behind and does not honor the academic expectations

<Warning>
  **Academic progress keeps the sports side going.** Continued participation in facility training and coaching depends on staying engaged academically. If a student falls too far behind, the school may pause training payments until they're back on pace. The intent is to support the student — not to take sports away as a punishment.
</Warning>
