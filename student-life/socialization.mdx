One of the most common questions families ask before enrolling is whether their child will have enough social connection in a virtual school. It's a fair question — and the honest answer is that Texas Sports Academy Online students often have *more* meaningful social interaction than they did in a traditional school setting, not less. Between daily live sessions, team training at local facilities, community events, and a statewide network of families, your child is far from isolated.

<Note>
**Addressing the isolation concern directly:** Texas Sports Academy Online is not a solo homeschool experience. Your child joins live collaborative sessions with age-group peers every school day, trains alongside teammates and coaches at a local facility, and belongs to an active community of families across Texas who share the same values around learning and athletics. Isolation is the exception, not the rule.
</Note>

## Two Types of Community Events

Texas Sports Academy Online events come in two flavors, and both are valuable:

**Staff-Hosted Events** are organized by the Texas Sports Academy team. Look for the **Admin**, **Community Manager**, or **Coach** badge next to the host's name. These events are structured, well-organized, and a great way for new families to get introduced to the community.

**Community-Led Events** are organized by families themselves — playdates, local meetups, regional get-togethers. These are especially powerful for building genuine peer friendships, because they're driven by the families who know each other best.

You can find both types at [sportsacademy.school/home/events](https://sportsacademy.school/home/events).

## A Flexible Schedule That Prioritizes Social Life

Because academics take roughly two hours a day, your family controls the rest. That means you can schedule your child's social activities — sports practice, community groups, faith communities, neighborhood play — **without working around a rigid school timetable**. The program is designed to open up your child's life, not lock it down.

## The Bigger Picture

Texas Sports Academy Online is a **community of families across Texas** united by shared values around learning and athletics. Your child isn't logging into an anonymous platform — they're joining a real network of students, parents, coaches, and guides who know each other by name, collaborate daily, and show up for each other at events throughout the year.

<Tip>
New to the community? Start by joining the regional chat room on the [community platform](https://sportsacademy.school/home/start) and introducing your family. Families in your area are already there — and many are eager to meet new neighbors with kids the same age.
</Tip>
