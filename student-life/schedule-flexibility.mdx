Texas Sports Academy Online was designed from the ground up for families whose lives don't fit inside a traditional 8-to-3 school day. Whether your child is a competitive athlete who travels for tournaments, a performer with rehearsals in the afternoon, or a family that simply values controlling your own time, the schedule bends to your life — not the other way around.

## No Fixed Login Time. Ever.

There is no required time to log on. Academics can happen in the morning, the afternoon, or the evening — your family decides. The only daily goal is **120 XP**, which represents roughly 120 minutes of focused, productive learning. Once your student hits 120 XP and goes green, they're done for the day.

Work is broken into **25-minute Pomodoro focus blocks**, so even busy days can be completed in structured, manageable chunks. No class periods. No attendance penalties. No teachers to email when you're traveling.

## Live Guide Sessions: You Pick the Time

Live Guide sessions run Monday through Friday over Zoom. Your family picks **one of three start times** each day:

| Start Time | Best For |
|---|---|
| **9:00 AM Central** | Families who prefer to finish academics early and have the afternoon free |
| **12:00 PM (Noon) Central** | Families with morning training or activities |
| **3:00 PM Central** | Families with full mornings and early afternoons committed to other activities |

If 9 AM doesn't work today, choose noon. If noon doesn't work tomorrow, choose 3 PM. The session time is your choice every day.

## Traveling for Tournaments? You're Covered.

Competitive athletes travel. That's not a bug — it's the whole point of this program.

<Tip>
**Work ahead before you leave.** If your child has a tournament or competition coming up, they can bank XP in the days before departure. Once they're **3 or more school days ahead** (~360 XP ahead of pace), the daily Guide session becomes optional for as long as they stay ahead — giving you a true competition week with zero academic pressure.
</Tip>

Here's how travel works in practice:

- **Before the trip:** Work ahead and build a cushion. The platform tracks pace, so you'll always know exactly where you stand.
- **On the road:** If your student has downtime — a hotel evening, a long drive with a hotspot — they can chip away at XP from anywhere with the MacBook and an internet connection.
- **After the trip:** If they took a full break, that's fine. Daily targets adjust slightly so your student can catch back up at a comfortable pace. This is not traditional "makeup work" — it's just continuing the path.

## Absences and Breaks Work Differently Here

Missing a day, a week, or even longer doesn't trigger the cascade of missed assignments and teacher emails that traditional school brings. Because learning is self-paced and mastery-based, your student simply picks up where they left off. There are no attendance penalties and no failing grades for missing days.

Your family can also **schedule your own days off and breaks in advance** — no permission slip required.

## Not Just for Athletes

The flexible schedule benefits any student whose life has demands that don't fit a standard school day:

- **Artists and performers** with morning or afternoon rehearsals
- **Young entrepreneurs** building businesses or pursuing passion projects
- **Gamers and creatives** who are most focused at non-traditional hours
- **Families who travel** for reasons beyond sports — extended family, adventure, seasonal living

## The Biometric Data Question

<Note>
Texas Sports Academy Online does **not** collect or store biometric data. The MacBook your student receives includes Apple's Touch ID fingerprint sensor, which is an optional device feature that stays entirely on the device — Texas Sports Academy never has access to it. You can enable or ignore Touch ID according to your own family's preference.
</Note>

## Two Working Parents? The Program Has You Covered.

<Note>
You do not need to be home teaching or supervising for this to work. The platform handles instruction. Live Guide sessions provide daily structure and human accountability. The parent dashboard gives you daily visibility into exactly what your child worked on and their grade-level progress — all without you needing to be in the room. Thousands of families with two working parents have made this model work seamlessly.
</Note>

## The Bottom Line

After roughly two hours of focused academics, **the rest of the day belongs to your family**. Training, errands, family time, social activities, faith communities, hobbies — you build the schedule that makes sense for your life. Texas Sports Academy Online fits into it.
