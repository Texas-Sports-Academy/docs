Your child's day at Texas Sports Academy Online is built around a simple idea: finish your focused academic work, demonstrate mastery, and the rest of the day belongs to you. There are no class periods to sit through, no homework to drag into the evening, and no one-size-fits-all schedule — just purposeful learning, real athletic development, and space for the life your family actually lives.

## How a Typical Day Flows

Every student's day moves through three natural phases: academics, training, and free time. Your family chooses whether academics happen in the morning, the afternoon, or the evening — whatever fits your schedule best.

| Phase | What Happens |
|---|---|
| **Academics** | ~2 hours of self-paced, personalized learning on the MacBook, broken into 25-minute Pomodoro focus blocks |
| **Sports Training** | Time at a local partner facility — rec center, YMCA, or sports complex — with coaches and peers |
| **Free Time** | Once your student "goes green" (achieves mastery for the day), the rest of the day is entirely theirs |

### The 120 XP Daily Goal

Each day has a target of **120 XP**, which represents roughly 120 minutes of productive, engaged learning. XP is earned by demonstrating understanding — not by sitting in front of a screen. When your child hits 120 XP and goes green, they're done. No homework. Not ever.

## Live Guide Sessions

Every school day (Monday–Friday), students join a live **Guide Session** over Zoom. These are collaborative work sessions — not lectures. Guides are present to support, answer questions, and facilitate group activities in breakout rooms. Students pick **one session per day** from three start times:

- **9:00 AM Central**
- **12:00 PM (Noon) Central**
- **3:00 PM Central**

Once a student is **3 or more school days ahead of pace** (~360 XP ahead), the daily Guide session becomes optional for as long as they stay ahead.

## Your Child's MacBook

Every student receives a **new Apple MacBook**. All learning software runs on it. It goes to the hotel, the tournament venue, the grandparents' house — wherever your student goes, their school goes with them.

## NIL Life Skills

Academic learning at Texas Sports Academy Online extends beyond core subjects. Every student works on **NIL Life Skills** woven into the curriculum:

- **Public Speaking** — Press conference scenarios that prepare student-athletes for the spotlight
- **Financial Literacy** — Real-world money skills built for athletes and young entrepreneurs
- **Grit & Feedback** — Learning how to receive, process, and grow from criticism

## What Your Child's First Week Looks Like

<Steps>
  <Step title="Day 1: Diagnostic Screeners (30–45 minutes)">
    On the first day, your student completes short diagnostic screeners in **math, reading, science, and language arts**. These take 30–45 minutes total and are not tests to pass or fail — they're maps.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Day 1: Knowledge Grade Assigned Per Subject">
    Based on the screeners, the platform identifies your child's actual knowledge level in each subject independently. A student might be working at a 7th-grade level in math and a 9th-grade level in reading — and that's perfectly fine.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Day 1: Personalized Learning Path Built">
    The system builds a customized learning path for each subject, starting exactly where your child is — not where their age says they should be.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Day 1: First Focused Session Begins">
    With paths in place, your student jumps into their first 25-minute Pomodoro block and begins earning XP that same day.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Days 2–5: Finding the Rhythm">
    Your child joins their first live Guide session, meets their Guide and classmates, and settles into the morning-afternoon-or-evening rhythm your family chose. By end of week one, most students have a clear routine and a real sense of their own pace.
  </Step>
</Steps>
