## What is AlphaWrite?

AlphaWrite is TSA's **writing** app for **grades 6–8**. Students build writing skills through **Sentences & Paragraphs** activities and longer **essays**, earning XP as they complete work to a high standard.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Writing practice" icon="pen">
    Sentence, paragraph, and essay activities that build composition skills.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Full-marks XP" icon="award">
    XP rewards **completed, high-quality** writing — an activity needs to reach full marks to earn its XP.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Purpose

- Build **writing skills** for grades 6–8 through structured Sentences & Paragraphs activities and essays.
- Reward **completed, high-quality** work rather than partial drafts.
- Report to TimeBack **natively** (first-party); XP posts in **under a minute**.

<Info>
  AlphaWrite is a **first-party app** — it is **not** in the third-party [Bridge](/learning-apps/timeback-mdx). Its XP model is stricter than most: an activity earns XP only when it reaches **full marks (100/100)**, and **retries earn progressively less**. See the [XP quick reference](#xp-quick-reference).
</Info>

<Info>
  Two things surprise families: an activity earns XP only at **full marks (100/100)**, and the **attempt multiplier** means retries are worth less — **1st attempt ×1.0, 2nd ×0.5, 3rd ×0.25, 4th\+ ×0**. _(Source: TimeBack XP rules; confirm the exact scoring/accuracy rule with an SME.)_
</Info>

### 1. A correct writing answer is scored as wrong

- **Type:** Product defect
- **Applications:** AlphaWrite _(also referred to in tickets as "Alpha Standardized Writing" — confirm naming)_
- **Symptom / verbatim:** Harrison — a **writing test scored a correct answer as wrong**.
- **Root cause:** Suspected grading defect in the writing/standardized-writing scoring — a valid response marked incorrect.

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm the pattern">
    Verify the response should have counted as correct but was marked wrong.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Capture the specifics">
    Note the prompt/question, what the student wrote, the expected answer, and a screenshot so the scoring can be reviewed and corrected.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reassure the student">
    Their work isn't lost — the item can be reviewed and the score corrected.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** The item is documented for review, and — if it's a scoring defect — flagged for a fix.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], thanks for flagging this, and sorry for the frustration! It sounds
like [Student]'s writing answer was marked wrong when it should have counted. Could
you send me the prompt, what [Student] wrote, and a screenshot? I'll get it reviewed
so the scoring can be checked and corrected. Their work isn't lost in the meantime —
thanks for your patience!
```

**Metadata:** Reported by Harrison · Severity **Medium–High**

### 2. No XP unless the activity reaches full marks

- **Type:** User education
- **Applications:** AlphaWrite
- **Symptom / verbatim:** _Anticipated from AlphaWrite's XP rules_ — "my child wrote a whole piece but got no XP."
- **Root cause:** AlphaWrite awards its 15 XP per activity only when the work reaches **full marks (100/100)** (with ≥80% accuracy). A draft that isn't yet at full marks earns no XP; 100% accuracy adds a 25% bonus.

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check the score">
    AlphaWrite needs a **100/100** on the activity for XP — confirm whether the piece reached full marks.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explain the bar">
    Writing activities award XP at full marks; a piece that isn't there yet can be **revised and resubmitted** to earn it (and top accuracy adds a 25% bonus).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Encourage revision">
    Have the student polish and complete the activity to full marks.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** XP posts when the activity reaches 100/100.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], good question! AlphaWrite awards its XP when an activity is
completed at full marks (a 100/100), so a writing piece that isn't quite there yet
won't add XP until it's finished to that level. The good news is [Student] can revise
and resubmit — reaching full marks posts the XP (and top accuracy adds a bonus).
Happy to help [Student] polish it if that would help!
```

**Metadata:** Grounded in XP rules; watch for tickets · Severity **Medium**

### 3. Less XP on repeated attempts

- **Type:** User education
- **Applications:** AlphaWrite
- **Symptom / verbatim:** _Anticipated from AlphaWrite's XP rules_ — "my child redid the activity but earned less XP."
- **Root cause:** An **attempt multiplier** reduces XP on retries: **1st attempt ×1.0, 2nd ×0.5, 3rd ×0.25, 4th and beyond ×0**. Later attempts earn progressively less by design.

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Explain the multiplier">
    The first attempt earns full XP; each retry earns less — about half on the second, a quarter on the third, and none from the fourth on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Encourage a careful first attempt">
    Doing the best work on the first try earns the most XP.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reassure">
    Earned XP is recorded on the account; nothing is lost.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** The XP matches the attempt number, and the parent understands why retries earn less.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], quick note on AlphaWrite XP — the first attempt at an activity
earns the full XP, and each retry after that earns a bit less (about half on the
second try, a quarter on the third). So if [Student] redid an activity a few times,
the later attempts add less. It's all working as intended, and their earned XP is
saved. Encouraging a careful first attempt earns the most!
```

**Metadata:** Grounded in XP rules; watch for tickets · Severity **Low–Medium**
