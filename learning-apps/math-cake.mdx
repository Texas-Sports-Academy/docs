## What is Math Cakes?

Math Cakes is a **mastery-based math practice app** in the TSA math suite. Students move through a tiered path for each skill — a **diagnostic** to place them, **tutorials** to teach, **practice** to build fluency, and a **mastery test** to prove the skill. Activities are tiered **plain → bronze → silver → gold**, with gold being graded practice and mastery. _(Structure inferred from Math Cakes' XP rules; confirm the exact student-facing flow with an SME.)_

## Purpose

- Deliver **skill-by-skill math mastery** rather than open-ended practice.
- Place students via a **diagnostic**, then move them through **tutorial → practice → mastery test** for each skill.
- Reward **mastery** (passing the gold mastery test) rather than time-on-task alone.

<Info>
  Because Math Cakes is **mastery-based**, XP is tied to _passing_, not just doing the work — a failed mastery test earns **0 XP**. This drives most "my child worked but got no XP" tickets on this app. See the [XP quick reference](#xp-quick-reference).
</Info>

## XP quick reference

Grounded in TimeBack's per-app XP rules. XP posts after a delay of **under 5 minutes**.

| Activity | XP | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Diagnostic | 1 XP per minute | Placement; time-based |
| Tutorial (plain / bronze / silver) | XP shown on the card | **First time only**; perfect score = **\+1 XP** |
| Practice (gold) | 2 XP | Perfect score = **\+1 XP** |
| Mastery Test (gold) | **Pass = 2 XP · Fail = 0 XP** | Perfect score = **\+1 XP** |

<Info>
  Two things surprise families: repeating a tutorial earns **no XP the second time** (first-time only), and **failing a mastery test earns 0 XP** even though the student did the work. Neither is a bug. _(Source: TimeBack XP rules.)_
</Info>

### 1. Course is too hard, with no way to adjust difficulty

- **Type:** Product limitation
- **Applications:** Math Cakes
- **Symptom / verbatim:**
  - Nonthapat, Napatt `[6/29]` — the math is too hard, and there is no visible way to adjust the level.
- **Root cause:** Math Cakes sets difficulty from the **diagnostic/placement**, not a manual difficulty slider. If placement is too high — or the student skipped the tutorials that scaffold a skill — practice and mastery tests will feel too hard.

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Explain how leveling works">
    Math Cakes is mastery-based: the level comes from the diagnostic, not a manual setting. There is no difficulty slider by design.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Route through tutorials first">
    Have the student complete the **tutorials** for the skill before practice/mastery — they scaffold the skill and make the mastery test achievable.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check placement">
    If it is consistently too hard across skills, the placement/grade may be set too high. Confirm the student's placement with their teacher.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Flag a misplacement">
    If placement looks wrong, note it for a placement review so the student can be re-leveled.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** The student can complete tutorials and pass a mastery test at their level, or a placement review is underway.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], thanks for letting us know Math Cakes feels too hard for
[Student]! A quick note on how it works: Math Cakes sets each skill's level from
the diagnostic, so there isn't a manual difficulty setting — but the tutorials are
there to teach a skill before the practice and mastery test, so having [Student]
work through those first usually helps it click. If it feels too hard across the
board, it may be a placement issue — I'll check [Student]'s placement with their
teacher and follow up. Thanks for flagging it!
```

**Metadata:** Reported `[6/29]` · Severity **Medium–High**

### 2. Family prefers "standard math" instead of Math Cakes

- **Type:** Product limitation
- **Applications:** Math Cakes
- **Symptom / verbatim:**
  - Alex — requests standard math instead of Math Cakes.
- **Root cause:** Math Cakes is the assigned mastery-based math approach; there isn't a self-serve option to swap it for a different math program per student.

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Acknowledge the preference">
    Recognize the request and explain that Math Cakes is the assigned math path, chosen for its skill-by-skill mastery approach.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explain the benefit">
    Mastery-based practice targets the specific skills a student hasn't yet mastered, rather than repeating what they already know.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log the feedback">
    Capture the preference as product feedback; program/curriculum changes are decided at the program level, not per ticket.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** The family understands why Math Cakes is assigned, and the preference is logged.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], thanks for sharing this! Math Cakes is [Student]'s assigned math
path right now — it's built around mastering one skill at a time, so it focuses on
exactly what [Student] hasn't mastered yet rather than repeating what they already
know. There isn't a way to swap it for a different math program from here, but I've
passed your preference along as feedback. Happy to help [Student] get more
comfortable with it in the meantime!
```

**Metadata:** Isolated · Severity **Medium**

### 3. No XP earned (failed mastery test or repeated tutorial)

- **Type:** User education
- **Applications:** Math Cakes
- **Symptom / verbatim:** Students report completing Math Cakes work but seeing no XP.
- **Root cause:** Math Cakes ties XP to **mastery**, not time. A **failed mastery test earns 0 XP**, and a **repeated tutorial earns no XP** (first-time only). Both look like "I did the work but got nothing."

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm what activity was done">
    Identify whether it was a tutorial (first-time only), practice (gold), or a mastery test.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explain the mastery rule">
    A **failed mastery test earns 0 XP**, and a **repeated tutorial earns no XP** — this is the app rule, not a malfunction.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Encourage the path to XP">
    XP comes from **passing** the gold practice/mastery test (2 XP, \+1 for a perfect score). Route the student back through the tutorial, then retry the mastery test.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Rule out a real gap">
    If the student **passed** and still has no XP after the ~5-minute delay, treat it as a sync issue (see the [TimeBack "No XP" triage](/learning/learning-apps/timeback)).
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** XP posts within ~5 minutes after the student passes a gold practice/mastery activity.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], good news — nothing's broken! Math Cakes gives XP for mastering
a skill, not just for time spent, so a couple of things can lead to 0 XP: a
mastery test that wasn't passed, or repeating a tutorial (those only give XP the
first time). The way to earn it is to work back through the tutorial and then pass
the gold practice/mastery test — that posts XP, usually within about 5 minutes. If
[Student] passed and still doesn't see it after that, let me know and I'll take a
closer look!
```

**Metadata:** Recurring XP pattern · Severity **Medium**
