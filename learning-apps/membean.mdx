## What is Membean?

Membean is a **vocabulary learning platform** that uses **adaptive spaced-repetition** — it reintroduces words over time so they stick in long-term memory. At TSA it's the vocab app for **grades 6–8**. Students learn in **training sessions** that mix **new words** and **review words**.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Spaced repetition" icon="repeat">
    Words come back at increasing intervals so they move into long-term memory — reviews are part of the design.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Session-based" icon="clock">
    Students train in sessions; Membean scores accuracy and productive minutes across the whole session.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Purpose

- Build **vocabulary** for grades 6–8 through adaptive, spaced-repetition training.
- Mix **new and review words** so learning sticks over time.
- Report per-session XP and progress into TimeBack.

<Info>
  Membean is a **third-party app** — it reports into TimeBack through the [Bridge](/learning-apps/timeback-mdx). Two things make its XP different from most apps: XP is **awarded at the end of a training session** (not during it), and it's **per word** (new words are worth more than reviews). See the [XP quick reference](#xp-quick-reference).
</Info>

## XP quick reference

Grounded in TimeBack's per-app XP rules.

| App | Accuracy needed | Typical delay | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Membean | 80% (whole session) | 2–5 min | Vocab (G6–8); **~0.75 XP per new word**, **~0.33 XP per review word**; **\+25%** for a 100% session; **XP posts at the end of the session** |

<Info>
  Below **80% session accuracy**, no XP posts. XP is calculated **when the session ends** (then a 2–5 minute delay), and **review words earn less than new words** by design. These three facts explain nearly every Membean XP question. _(Source: TimeBack XP rules.)_
</Info>

### 1. No XP until the training session is finished

- **Type:** User education
- **Applications:** Membean
- **Symptom / verbatim:** _Anticipated from Membean's XP rules_ — "my child has been training but no XP has shown up."
- **Root cause:** Membean calculates XP **when a training session ends**, not continuously — and then it posts after a **2–5 minute** delay. Mid-session, no XP will show.

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm the session finished">
    Membean posts XP only when a **training session is completed** — check whether the student is still mid-session.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explain end-of-session posting">
    XP is tallied at the end of the session and then posts after a **2–5 minute** delay.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Finish and wait">
    Have the student complete the session, then check back after a few minutes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Rule out accuracy">
    If the session is finished and XP still doesn't post, check the session accuracy ([Concern 2](#2-no-xp-when-session-accuracy-is-below-80)).
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** XP posts within 2–5 minutes after the student completes a session.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], good news — nothing's wrong! Membean adds up XP at the end of a
training session rather than during it, and it takes a few minutes to post. So if
[Student] is mid-session (or just finished), the XP will appear shortly after they
complete the session. Have them wrap up the session and check back in a few minutes —
let me know if it still doesn't show and I'll look closer!
```

**Metadata:** Grounded in XP rules; watch for tickets · Severity **Medium**

### 2. No XP when session accuracy is below 80%

- **Type:** User education
- **Applications:** Membean
- **Symptom / verbatim:** _Anticipated from Membean's XP rules_ — "my child finished a session but got no XP."
- **Root cause:** Membean requires **80% accuracy across the whole session** to award XP. A finished session below 80% earns **0 XP** — the app rule, not a malfunction. A perfect (100%) session adds a **25% bonus**.

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm the session finished">
    First rule out a still-in-progress session (see [Concern 1](#1-no-xp-until-the-training-session-is-finished)).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check session accuracy">
    Membean needs **≥80% accuracy** for the session. Below that, no XP posts even after finishing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explain and encourage">
    Slowing down to answer carefully lifts accuracy above the bar — and a 100% session earns a 25% bonus.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** XP posts once the student completes a session at ≥80% accuracy.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], quick tip on Membean XP — it needs the session's accuracy to be
80% or higher to award XP. If [Student]'s session came in below that, it won't add XP
even after finishing. Slowing down just a little to answer carefully usually does the
trick (and a perfect session actually earns a 25% bonus!). Happy to help if [Student]
finds the words tricky!
```

**Metadata:** Grounded in XP rules; watch for tickets · Severity **Medium**

### 3. XP seems low for the time spent

- **Type:** User education
- **Applications:** Membean
- **Symptom / verbatim:** _Anticipated from Membean's XP rules_ — "the same amount of time earned much less XP than before."
- **Root cause:** XP is **per word**, and **review words (~0.33 XP) earn less than new words (~0.75 XP)** — so a review-heavy session earns less even for the same time. Reviews are core to spaced repetition, so this is expected. Membean also only credits **productive** practice (rushed or low-quality "dubious" minutes don't count).

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Explain per-word XP">
    New words earn more (~0.75 XP) than review words (~0.33 XP), so a session that's mostly review earns less — even for the same amount of time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explain quality matters">
    Membean credits **productive** practice; rushed or guessed ("dubious") minutes don't count as real training.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Coach toward XP">
    Steady, careful work — especially on new words — earns the most, and a 100% session adds a 25% bonus.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** XP is consistent with the mix of new vs. review words and focused minutes in the session.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], great question! Membean gives more XP for brand-new words (about
0.75 each) than for review words (about 0.33 each), so a session that's mostly review
earns less even for the same time — that's expected, since revisiting words is how
they stick. It also only counts focused practice, so quick guessing doesn't add up.
Steady, careful work on new words earns the most (and a 100% session adds a 25%
bonus). Their progress is always saved!
```

**Metadata:** Grounded in XP rules; watch for tickets · Severity **Low–Medium**
