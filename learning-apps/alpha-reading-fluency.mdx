Alpha Reading Fluency is a **reading app for young students** focused on the foundational skill of reading **accurately and smoothly out loud**. The app listens as your child reads aloud and provides real-time support, building the automatic decoding that makes later reading comprehension possible. It is designed with early and developing readers in mind, including built-in accessibility for students with dyslexia.

In simple terms: this is the **"learn to read"** stage — decoding and oral fluency — that comes _before_ a student moves on to comprehension-focused work.

## What It Is

- **Oral reading fluency.** Students read words and passages aloud; the app uses speech technology to listen, give feedback, and help correct mistakes in the moment.
- **Built for early and struggling readers.** A friendly, kid-focused experience that meets each child at their level.
- **Dyslexia-friendly by design.** The app uses the **OpenDyslexic** typeface to make text easier to read for students with dyslexia.

## How It Connects to Alpha

Alpha Reading Fluency is not a standalone product — it is one app inside the broader Alpha learning ecosystem, connected in three ways:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Same account (Timeback login)">
    Students sign in with **"Continue with Timeback"** — the same Timeback identity that powers the parent dashboard and student logins across the program. One account reaches every Alpha app, including this one. See [Account Access](/academics/account-access) for how Timeback logins work.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Same placement model">
    The app uses **diagnostic screeners** to find where your child actually is, then starts instruction there — the same **Knowledge Grade** and mastery-based approach used throughout the [2 Hour Learning](/academics/2-hour-learning) model.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Its place in the reading pipeline">
    Alpha Reading Fluency is the **foundational fluency stage** of the reading sequence. Once a student reads accurately and smoothly, they progress to comprehension-focused work in **AlphaRead**, which steadily increases article difficulty toward grade-level and SAT goals. Fluency first, comprehension next — all under the same mastery system described in the [Curriculum](/academics/curriculum) overview.
  </Step>
</Steps>

</Note>
