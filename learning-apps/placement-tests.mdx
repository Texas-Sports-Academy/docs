## What is placement testing?

Placement testing is how a new student's **starting level is set in each subject**. It runs early, before daily coursework, and has two parts:

- **MAP screener** — a first-day **diagnostic** (NWEA). It gauges level and is **not** a pass/fail test. _(Different from the NWEA MAP growth test the school runs three times a year — see [Account & Access](/academics/account-access).)_
- **Placement / Alpha tests** — the grade-level tests, delivered through the **Alpha Test Player**, that confirm the student's level per subject and unlock coursework.

<Info>
  **Where this sits in the journey:** **MAP → Placement → Courses.** During placement the dashboard is quiet by design (the student is testing, not doing daily coursework), which is itself a common ticket — see [TimeBack Concern 1](/learning-apps/timeback-mdx). Placement / Alpha tests **do award XP** on completion (e.g., 60–140 XP for a math grade-level test; **test-outs earn 2×**); the MAP screener is diagnostic. _(Source: TimeBack XP rules; confirm MAP-XP with an SME.)_
</Info>

### 1. Password rejected at sign-in

- **Type:** User education
- **Applications:** Alpha Test Player (placement)
- **Symptom / verbatim:** Fariha Fatima — the Alpha Test Player **rejected the standard password**; **"Continue with Google"** was required.
- **Root cause:** The test player signs in via **Google SSO** for these accounts — the standard password won't work. Students authenticate with their **school Google account** (TimeBack provisions each student a Google Workspace account).

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Use 'Continue with Google'">
    Have the student choose **"Continue with Google"** instead of typing a password.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use the school Google account">
    Confirm they're signing in with their **TimeBack-provisioned school Google account**, not a personal one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check provisioning if Google fails">
    If Google sign-in also fails, the account may not be provisioned yet — confirm the student has an active Google Workspace/TimeBack account (see [rostering](/operations/enrollment/readiness-and-rostering)).
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** The student signs in via Google and reaches their placement test.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], happy to help [Student] get into their placement test! This one
signs in with Google rather than a typed password — on the sign-in screen, choose
"Continue with Google" and use [Student]'s school Google account. That should take
them straight in. If the Google sign-in doesn't work either, let me know and I'll
make sure their account is fully set up.
```

**Metadata:** Reported by Fariha Fatima · Severity **Medium**

### 2. A subject is locked / unavailable

- **Type:** User education
- **Applications:** Alpha Test Player (placement)
- **Symptom / verbatim:** Alexandria Jan `[6/29]` — science and writing were **unavailable**; unlocking them requires **90%** on lower-grade tests.
- **Root cause:** Subjects and levels **unlock progressively** — a student must pass the lower-grade placement tests (around **90%**) before higher-grade or additional-subject tests open. A locked subject is expected, not broken.

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Explain the unlock logic">
    Subjects/levels open as the student passes the lower-grade tests (around **90%**). A locked subject means the prerequisite hasn't been cleared yet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Point to the open test">
    Have the student complete the **currently-available** placement test first — that's what unlocks the next one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reassure it's automatic">
    Locked subjects unlock on their own as the student progresses — nothing needs to be requested.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** The locked subject unlocks after the student passes the prerequisite test.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], good question! Those subjects aren't broken — they unlock as
[Student] passes the earlier-grade placement tests (usually around 90%). So the best
next step is to finish the test that's currently open, and science/writing will
unlock automatically from there. Let me know if one doesn't open after [Student]
passes the earlier one and I'll take a look!
```

**Metadata:** Reported by Alexandria Jan `[6/29]` · Severity **Medium–High**

### 3. Placement starts too low or re-tests already-passed grades

- **Type:** User education
- **Applications:** Alpha Test Player (placement)
- **Symptom / verbatim:**
  - Muhammad Ahmed — reading placement **began at kindergarten**; asked if that was expected.
  - Napatt / Nuttapone — **passed grade 3 math but was still asked to complete grades 1 and 2**; a retry returned an error.
  - Zoey Hsu — asked whether **failing the grade 6 placement** would start her at grade 6 or grade 5.
- **Root cause:** Placement **confirms foundational skills**, so it can start lower or re-check earlier grades before settling the level — this is by design (the [MAP → Placement → Courses sequence](/learning-apps/timeback-mdx)). Failing a level places the student at the appropriate lower level; passing advances them. _The "retry returned an error" (Napatt) may be a separate defect — capture it._

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Explain how placement finds the level">
    Placement checks foundational skills, so it may start lower or re-confirm earlier grades before locking in the level. Starting low isn't a mistake.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set expectations on the result">
    Failing a level simply places the student at the right lower level; passing advances them. It's a one-time sequence, then daily coursework begins at the confirmed level.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Rule out a defect">
    If a retry **returns an error** or the sequence keeps re-testing an already-passed grade, capture the student, subject, and error so it can be looked at.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** The student completes the placement sequence and lands at the correct level, or a stuck/error case is documented.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], great question! Placement starts by confirming the building-block
skills, so it can begin a bit lower or double-check earlier grades before it settles
on [Student]'s level — that's normal, not a mistake. Whatever they pass or don't
determines exactly where their coursework begins, and it's a one-time setup. If a
test throws an error when [Student] retries, send me the details and I'll get it
checked.
```

**Metadata:** Reported by Muhammad, Napatt/Nuttapone, Zoey · Severity **Medium–High**

### 4. Test submits early or gets stuck on "Incomplete"

- **Type:** Product defect
- **Applications:** Alpha Test Player (placement)
- **Symptom / verbatim:**
  - Elliott — a reading test **submitted at question 2 of 36**.
  - Alexandria — a test was **stuck on "Incomplete."**
- **Root cause:** Suspected defect — a test submitting prematurely or failing to register completion.

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm the symptom">
    Check whether the test **ended early** (e.g., submitted at Q2 of 36) or shows **"Incomplete"** despite the student finishing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reload and re-enter">
    Have the student log out and back in, then reopen the test from the **Placement** menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Don't rely on an early submission">
    If it submitted early, the test likely needs to be **reset/re-assigned** rather than counted — don't treat the partial result as the placement.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Capture the specifics">
    Note the student, subject, where it stopped (question count), and a screenshot so it can be reset and fixed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** The student can complete the full test and it registers as complete.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], thanks for flagging this, and sorry for the hassle! It sounds like
the test ended before [Student] finished (or won't move off "Incomplete"). First,
could they log out and back in and reopen it from the Placement menu? If it still
submits early or sticks, don't worry — we won't count a partial test as their
placement. Send me the subject and roughly where it stopped, and I'll get it reset.
```

**Metadata:** Reported by Elliott, Alexandria · Severity **High**

### 5. Test screen is blank or won't load

- **Type:** Product defect
- **Applications:** Alpha Test Player (placement)
- **Symptom / verbatim:** _Reported pattern_ — "blank or locked tests" that won't display.
- **Root cause:** Suspected loading/display defect (a blank screen) — distinct from a subject being **intentionally locked** (see [Concern 2](#2-a-subject-is-locked--unavailable)).

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Rule out an intentional lock">
    Confirm it's a **blank / won't-load** screen, not a subject that's locked until a prerequisite is passed (that's [Concern 2](#2-a-subject-is-locked--unavailable)).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reload / try another browser">
    Refresh or hard-reload, try a different browser, and check the internet connection.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Re-enter via the Placement menu">
    Go back to the **Placement** menu and reopen the test.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Capture specifics if it persists">
    Note the student, the test, and a screenshot so the loading issue can be fixed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** The test loads and the student can begin.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], sorry the test won't come up! A couple of quick things: have
[Student] refresh the page (or try a different browser), then reopen the test from
the Placement menu. If it's a specific subject that's greyed out rather than blank,
that's usually just locked until an earlier test is passed. If it's still blank after
a refresh, send me a screenshot and I'll get it looked into.
```

**Metadata:** Reported pattern (blank/locked tests) · Severity **Medium**
