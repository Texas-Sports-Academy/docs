## What is VocabLoco?

VocabLoco (also called **VocabClass**) is a **spelling and vocabulary** program with **game-based exercises**. Students work through **word lists** in different activity types (vocabulary, spelling, quizzes), and the app layers on **gamification** — game coins, badges, contests, and a virtual pet. At TSA it's the vocab app for **grades 4–5** and a **hole-filling** option for **grades 9–10**.

  </Card>

  <Card title="Game-based rewards" icon="gamepad">

## Purpose

- Build **spelling and vocabulary** for grades 4–5 (and hole-filling for 9–10) through game-based practice.
- Keep students engaged with **gamification** (coins, badges, contests, virtual pet).
- Report per-activity XP and progress into TimeBack.

<Info>
  VocabLoco is a **third-party app** — it reports into TimeBack through the [Bridge](/learning-apps/timeback-mdx). Two things drive its tickets: **repeating an already-completed lesson earns no XP**, and the app's **in-game coins/badges are not the same as TimeBack XP**. See the [XP quick reference](#xp-quick-reference).
</Info>

### 1. No XP when redoing an already-completed lesson

- **Type:** User education
- **Applications:** VocabLoco
- **Symptom / verbatim:** _Anticipated from VocabLoco's XP rules_ — "my child did VocabLoco but earned no XP."
- **Root cause:** VocabLoco awards XP for **new** work only — **previously-completed lessons don't award XP** again. Redoing a finished word list or activity earns 0 XP.

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check whether it was a repeat">
    Confirm whether the student redid a **word list/activity they'd already completed** — repeats don't earn XP.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explain the rule">
    XP comes from **new** activities, not from redoing completed ones — this is by design, not a malfunction.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Point to new work">
    Have the student move on to a **new** assigned activity or word list to start earning XP again (5 XP per activity).
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** XP posts when the student completes a new (not-previously-done) activity at ≥80%.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], good news — nothing's broken! VocabLoco only awards XP for new
activities, so if [Student] went back over a word list or lesson they'd already
finished, it won't add XP the second time. Moving on to a new assigned activity will
start earning XP again (about 5 XP per activity at 80%+). Let me know if a brand-new
activity still doesn't award anything and I'll check!
```

**Metadata:** Grounded in XP rules; watch for tickets · Severity **Medium**

### 2. No XP when activity accuracy is below 80%

- **Type:** User education
- **Applications:** VocabLoco
- **Symptom / verbatim:** _Anticipated from VocabLoco's XP rules_ — "my child finished an activity but got no XP."
- **Root cause:** VocabLoco awards its **5 XP per activity** only at **≥80% accuracy**. Below that, no XP posts (review tests award 0.5 XP per correct word, also at 80%).

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm the activity completed">
    Verify the student finished a VocabLoco activity (and that it wasn't a repeat — see [Concern 1](#1-no-xp-when-redoing-an-already-completed-lesson)).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the accuracy">
    An activity needs **≥80% accuracy** to award its 5 XP. Below that, no XP posts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explain and encourage a retry">
    A retry aiming for 80%\+ will post the XP (within the 1–3 minute delay).
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** XP posts once the student completes a new activity at ≥80% accuracy.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], quick note on VocabLoco XP — an activity needs to be completed at
80% accuracy or higher to award its XP. If [Student]'s session came in below that, it
won't add XP this time. A quick retry aiming for 80%+ should do it. Happy to help if
the words are giving [Student] trouble!
```

**Metadata:** Grounded in XP rules; watch for tickets · Severity **Medium**

### 3. In-app coins/badges aren't the same as TimeBack XP

- **Type:** User education
- **Applications:** VocabLoco
- **Symptom / verbatim:** _Anticipated from VocabLoco's gamification_ — "my child earned lots of coins/badges but barely any XP."
- **Root cause:** VocabLoco has its own **gamification** — game coins, badges, and a virtual pet — which is **separate** from TimeBack XP. Earning coins is not the same as earning XP; TimeBack XP comes from completing **new activities at ≥80%**.

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Distinguish the two reward systems">
    VocabLoco's coins, badges, and virtual pet are **in-app rewards**; TimeBack XP is separate and tracks learning progress.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explain how XP is earned">
    XP comes from completing **new activities at ≥80% accuracy** (about 5 XP each) — not from collecting coins.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reassure both are working">
    Coins and XP simply measure different things; neither being high or low means the other is broken.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** The parent understands coins ≠ XP, and XP posts from qualifying activities.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], great question! VocabLoco has its own fun rewards — game coins,
badges, and the virtual pet — and those are separate from the XP [Student] earns
toward their goals. The coins are just part of the game; the XP comes from completing
new activities at 80%+ accuracy (about 5 XP each). Both are working — they just
measure different things. Let me know if you'd like me to check [Student]'s XP for a
specific activity!
```

**Metadata:** Grounded in XP rules \+ gamification; watch for tickets · Severity **Medium**
