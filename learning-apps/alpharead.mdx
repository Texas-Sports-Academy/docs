## Subjects & Grades

- **Reading** — Grades 3–12

## XP Rule

- XP per article as shown in Timeback
- Fluency recording: 5 XP per recording
- Comprehension quiz: 5 XP per quiz

## Accuracy Threshold

- 80% on articles
- ≥75% of target reading fluency for the recording XP
- 75% on the comprehension quiz

## What is AlphaRead?

AlphaRead is TSA's **core reading app**, used across **grades 3–12**. Students read **articles** and answer **comprehension questions**, earning XP for each article they complete accurately.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Reading comprehension" icon="book-open">
    Students read articles and answer questions that build reading comprehension.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Per-article XP" icon="newspaper">
    Each article has its own XP value, shown in TimeBack, earned by passing its comprehension.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Purpose

- Build **reading comprehension** for grades 3–12 through leveled articles.
- Reward **accurate comprehension**, not just time reading.
- Report to TimeBack **natively** (first-party); XP posts in **under a minute**.

<Info>
  AlphaRead is a **first-party app** — it is **not** in the third-party [Bridge](/learning-apps/timeback-mdx). XP is earned **per article** when its comprehension is completed at **≥80% accuracy** — reading time alone doesn't post XP. See the [XP quick reference](#xp-quick-reference).
</Info>

### 1. No XP for reading time

- **Type:** User education
- **Applications:** AlphaRead _(reading app; app attribution to confirm on the ticket below)_
- **Symptom / verbatim:** Nonthapat / Napatt — reported **reading time completed with no XP awarded**. _(Confirm this was AlphaRead vs. another reading app.)_
- **Root cause:** AlphaRead awards XP **per article, when its comprehension is completed at ≥80% accuracy** — **reading time alone doesn't earn XP**, and a comprehension below 80% earns none.

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm the comprehension was completed">
    Check whether the student finished the article's **questions**, not just read the article.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the accuracy">
    The comprehension needs **≥80% accuracy** to award the article's XP.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explain and encourage a retry">
    Reading time on its own doesn't post XP — completing the questions at 80%\+ does.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** XP posts when the student completes an article's comprehension at ≥80% accuracy.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], good news — nothing's broken! AlphaRead awards XP when [Student]
completes an article's questions at 80% accuracy or higher — reading the article on
its own (or scoring below 80%) won't post XP. A quick retry aiming for 80%+ on the
comprehension should do it. Happy to help if the passages are tricky!
```

**Metadata:** Reported by Nonthapat/Napatt (reading; confirm app) · Severity **Medium**

### 2. XP varies from article to article

- **Type:** User education
- **Applications:** AlphaRead
- **Symptom / verbatim:** _Anticipated from AlphaRead's XP rules_ — "some articles earned more XP than others."
- **Root cause:** XP is set **per article** (and shown in TimeBack), so different articles are worth different amounts — a shorter or easier one earns less than a longer one. This variation is expected.

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Explain per-article XP">
    Each article has its own XP value, visible in TimeBack — so the amount varies by article.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Note length/level">
    Longer or higher-level articles are generally worth more than shorter ones.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reassure">
    Earned XP is recorded on the account; the variation is by design.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** The XP matches the article's stated value in TimeBack.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], great question! In AlphaRead the XP is set per article — you can
see each article's value in TimeBack — so different articles are worth different
amounts, and a shorter or easier one earns less than a longer one. That variation is
expected. Their earned XP is always saved!
```

**Metadata:** Grounded in XP rules; watch for tickets · Severity **Low–Medium**
