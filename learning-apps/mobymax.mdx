- Language: 5 XP per lesson; 2 XP per lesson test

- Language: 80% on lessons; 100% on lesson tests

## What is MobyMax?

MobyMax is a **multi-subject adaptive learning platform (K–12)** with 25\+ curriculum modules — Math, Science, Language, Reading, Writing, Phonics, Social Studies, Vocabulary, and Spelling. It finds a student's gaps with adaptive **"Find & Fix"** and assigns targeted skills to close them.

At TSA, MobyMax is used mainly for **Science, Language, and Reading**, often as a **hole-filling** (supplemental) app alongside the core subject apps.

## Purpose

- Provide **adaptive, gap-filling practice** across multiple subjects — at TSA primarily **Science, Language, and Reading**.
- Use **"Find & Fix"** to diagnose and assign the specific skills a student is missing.
- Report per-subject XP and completion into TimeBack.

<Info>
  MobyMax is a **third-party app** — it reports into TimeBack through the [Bridge](/learning-apps/timeback-mdx) on a ~3-minute poll cycle, so XP lands in about **1–3 minutes**. Its XP amount **depends on the subject and activity**, which is a common source of "the XP seems off" questions.
</Info>

## XP quick reference

Grounded in TimeBack's per-app XP rules. MobyMax requires **≥80% accuracy** to award XP in every subject.

| App | Accuracy needed | Typical delay | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| MobyMax | 80% | 1–3 min (~3 min poll) | **Science:** 13 XP (G3–5), 25 XP (G6–8) · **Language:** 5 XP/lesson \+ 2 XP for a perfect test · **Reading:** 2–13 XP per skill |

<Info>
  Below 80% accuracy, **no XP posts at all** — the app rule, not a bug. This is the single most common MobyMax ticket (e.g., a completed science module showing 0 XP). _(Source: TimeBack XP rules.)_
</Info>

### 1. No XP on a module when accuracy is below 80%

- **Type:** User education
- **Applications:** MobyMax (all subjects; most often Science)
- **Symptom / verbatim:** Zoey Hsu — MobyMax **science** module awarded **no XP**.
- **Root cause:** MobyMax requires **≥80% accuracy** to award XP in every subject. A completed module below 80% earns **0 XP** — the work happened, but the accuracy bar wasn't met. This is the app rule, not a malfunction (see the [XP quick reference](#xp-quick-reference)). **Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm the work and subject">
    Verify the student completed a MobyMax module and note the subject (Science, Language, or Reading).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the accuracy">
    MobyMax requires **≥80% accuracy** in every subject. Below that, **0 XP** posts — the work happened, but the accuracy bar wasn't met.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explain and encourage a retry">
    This is the app rule, not a malfunction. Reaching 80%\+ on a retry posts the XP (within the 1–3 minute delay).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Rule out a real gap">
    If accuracy was ≥80% and XP is still missing after the ~3-minute poll, treat it as a sync issue (see the [TimeBack "No XP" triage](/learning-apps/timeback-mdx)).
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** XP posts within 1–3 minutes once the student reaches 80%\+, or the parent understands the accuracy rule.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], good news — [Student]'s work went through, nothing's broken!
MobyMax only awards XP when accuracy is 80% or higher, in every subject. This
[subject] module came in below that, so it didn't add XP this time. If [Student]
retries and reaches 80%+, the XP will post within a few minutes. Happy to walk
through the tricky skill with you if that would help!
```

**Metadata:** Reported by Zoey Hsu (science) · Severity **Medium–High**

### 2. XP seems inconsistent (varies by subject and activity)

- **Type:** User education
- **Applications:** MobyMax (Science, Language, Reading)
- **Symptom / verbatim:** _Anticipated from MobyMax's XP rules_ — "the same amount of work earned very different XP."
- **Root cause:** MobyMax XP **depends on the subject and activity**: Science is 13 XP (G3–5) or 25 XP (G6–8) per lesson, Language is 5 XP per lesson (plus 2 XP for a perfect test), and Reading is 2–13 XP per skill. Different modules earning different XP is expected, not a bug. **Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify the subject and activity">
    Different subjects and activities award different XP — check what the student actually worked on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explain the amounts">
    Science is 13 XP (G3–5) or 25 XP (G6–8) per lesson; Language is 5 XP per lesson (plus 2 XP for a perfect test); Reading is 2–13 XP per skill.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Normalize the variation">
    Different modules earning different XP is expected — the number reflects the subject and activity, not a bug.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reassure">
    Earned XP is recorded on the account; nothing is lost.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** The XP matches the subject/activity worked on, and the parent understands it varies by module.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], great question! MobyMax gives different XP depending on the
subject and activity — for example, a science lesson is worth more than a single
reading skill, and a perfect language test earns a small bonus. So the same amount
of time can add different XP depending on what [Student] worked on. It's all
expected, and their earned XP is safely recorded. Let me know if you'd like me to
check a specific module!
```

**Metadata:** Grounded in XP rules; watch for tickets · Severity **Medium**
