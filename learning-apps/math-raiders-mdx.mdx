## What is Math Raiders?

Math Raiders (built by **Playcademy**) is TSA's **Fast Math** app for **grades 3–8** — a game-based way to build math-fact **fluency and speed**. Students play short **"raids"** (1–2 minute mini-sessions, max ~2.5 min) answering math facts as quickly and accurately as they can.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Fluency through raids" icon="gauge-high">
    Short, fast raids build automatic recall of math facts — speed and accuracy both matter.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Effort-based XP" icon="bolt">
    XP rewards **focused, engaged** play — not just time with the app open.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Purpose

- Build **math-fact fluency** (fast, accurate recall) for grades 3–8, alongside the core math app.
- Reward **effort and engagement**, not idle time — XP scales with how focused the play is.
- Report to TimeBack **natively** (first-party); XP posts in about **30–60 seconds**.

<Info>
  Math Raiders is a **first-party app** — it is **not** in the third-party [Bridge](/learning-apps/timeback-mdx). Its XP model is unusual: it depends on **accuracy, speed, and engagement per raid**, which is the source of most "why so little XP?" questions. See the [XP quick reference](#xp-quick-reference).
</Info>

## XP quick reference

Grounded in TimeBack's per-app XP rules.

| App | Accuracy needed | Typical delay | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Math Raiders (Playcademy) | 80% per raid (\+ ≥2 correct/min) | 30–60 sec | First-party Fast Math (G3–8); **1 XP per minute of focused play**, scaled by an engagement multiplier |

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What counts as 'focused' play" icon="crosshairs">
    A raid earns XP only when it is **focused**: at least **80% accuracy** on that raid **and** a minimum of **2 correct questions per minute**. Below that, the raid doesn't earn its full XP.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="The engagement multiplier" icon="sliders">
    XP per raid is scaled against the student's **own best pace** (CQPM — correct questions per minute):

    - **Floor** = 25% of the student's best CQPM (minimum 2).
    - **0–29% of floor:** 0% XP (treated as AFK / not engaged).
    - **30–100% of floor:** XP scales linearly (e.g., 75% of floor → 75% XP).
    - **Above 100% of floor:** capped at 100% XP (no extra credit for going faster than your best).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### 1. Time spent in the app but little or no XP

- **Type:** User education
- **Applications:** Math Raiders
- **Symptom / verbatim:** _Anticipated from Math Raiders' XP rules_ — "my child was in Math Raiders for a while but earned almost no XP."
- **Root cause:** XP only accrues for **focused** raids — **≥80% accuracy** _and_ **≥2 correct questions/minute**. If the student is idle, distracted, or answering below the engagement **floor** (25% of their best pace), the raid earns **0% XP** (treated as AFK). Time with the app open is not enough on its own.

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Explain XP is effort-based">
    Math Raiders rewards **focused raids**, not time with the app open — a raid the student wasn't really engaged in can come back with little or no XP.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the two requirements">
    A raid earns XP only at **≥80% accuracy** _and_ **≥2 correct questions/minute**. Below the engagement floor it's treated as idle (0 XP).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Coach the play pattern">
    Encourage the student to stay locked in and answer steadily for the full 1–2 minutes of each short raid — that's what posts the XP.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Rule out a real gap">
    If the student was clearly focused and accurate but still shows 0 XP after ~1 minute, note it for a closer look (see the [TimeBack "No XP" triage](/learning-apps/timeback-mdx)).
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** Focused raids post XP within ~30–60 seconds, and the parent understands why idle time earns none.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], good question! Math Raiders is a little different from the other
apps — it rewards focused, engaged play rather than just time with the app open. A
short "raid" earns XP when [Student] is answering accurately (about 80%+) and
keeping up a steady pace; if it's mostly idle or very slow, that raid can come back
with little or no XP. The good news is nothing's broken — a few focused raids will
post XP within a minute or so. Let me know if [Student] was clearly engaged and
still saw nothing, and I'll take a closer look!
```

**Metadata:** Grounded in XP rules; watch for tickets · Severity **Medium**

### 2. XP varies a lot session to session

- **Type:** User education
- **Applications:** Math Raiders
- **Symptom / verbatim:** _Anticipated from Math Raiders' XP rules_ — "some days \[Student\] earns lots of XP and other days barely any for the same time."
- **Root cause:** The **engagement multiplier** scales each raid's XP between **30% and 100%** based on the student's pace relative to their **own best** (CQPM). A slower or less-focused session earns proportionally less; going _faster_ than their best doesn't earn extra (capped at 100%). So XP tracks **effort and focus**, not just minutes.

**Resolution steps**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Explain the multiplier">
    Each raid's XP scales with the student's pace compared to their **own best** — so a slower or more distracted session earns proportionally less than a locked-in one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Normalize the variation">
    Day-to-day differences are expected, not a bug. Going _faster_ than their personal best doesn't add extra (it's capped at 100%).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Frame it positively">
    Steady, focused practice earns the most XP and raises their pace over time — which is the whole point of a fluency app.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reassure">
    Earned XP is recorded on the account; nothing is lost between sessions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Verify resolved:** The parent understands XP tracks effort/pace, and a session's XP is consistent with how focused it was.

**Canned response**

```text
Hi [Parent name], great observation! Math Raiders scales XP to how focused and quick
each raid is compared to [Student]'s own best pace — so a really locked-in session
earns more than a slower or more distracted one, even for the same amount of time.
That day-to-day variation is expected, not a glitch (and going faster than their
personal best doesn't add extra — it's capped). Steady, focused practice earns the
most and builds their fluency over time. Their earned XP is always saved!
```

**Metadata:** Grounded in XP rules; watch for tickets · Severity **Medium**
