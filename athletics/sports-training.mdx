Sports training at a partner facility of your choice is a core part of the Texas Sports Academy Online program — it is included in your tuition, not an optional add-on. Whether your student is a competitive athlete, a dancer, a martial artist, or a rock climber, the program is built around giving them time and structured support to pursue their passion every single day.

## What sports are covered

Texas Sports Academy Online is sport-agnostic. There is no approved-sports list — if a qualified partner facility offers it, it counts. Sports and activities students currently train in include:

If your student's sport or activity is not listed above, that does not mean it is excluded — it means you may need to [request a facility](#requesting-a-facility) that offers it.

## How facility training works

Each student trains at **one partner facility at a time**, and that facility's training cost is covered by tuition. A few things to know:

- You can change facilities **month to month** — you are not locked in.
- If you have **multiple children**, each child can train at a different facility simultaneously.
- You select the facility; Texas Sports Academy Online handles the billing relationship with the partner facility directly.

## Requesting a facility

<Tip>
  If your preferred gym, rec center, or training facility is not on the partner list yet, you can request it directly from the [facilities page](https://sportsacademy.school/facilities). The Texas Sports Academy team will reach out to the facility, run background checks, confirm they have a structured training plan, and verify they can invoice through the program. Approval is not guaranteed — facilities go through a qualification process — but submitting the request is how the network grows.
</Tip>

## Virtual training platform

For students in areas without a nearby partner facility, or those who prefer to train at home, Texas Sports Academy Online also provides access to an **exclusive virtual training platform** packed with programs developed by professional athletes. This platform is available only to enrolled students.

## Is training required?

No. Sports training at a partner facility is an **option, not a mandate**. The core benefit of Texas Sports Academy Online is the flexible schedule — students who complete academics in roughly two hours each day gain 4+ extra hours daily to pursue whatever matters most to them. How they use that time is up to the family.

## Family referral program

When you refer another family to Texas Sports Academy Online and both families enroll and select a training facility, **both families earn a free group training session** at a facility of their choice.

## UIL sports eligibility

<Note>
  Texas Sports Academy Online is a **private school**. Students enrolled here are **not eligible to compete in UIL (University Interscholastic League) sports**, which are governed by Texas public school rules. However, students can — and do — compete in club leagues, AAU, travel leagues, and most homeschool leagues. If you are unsure whether a specific league allows Texas Sports Academy Online students, confirm directly with that league's director before enrolling.
</Note>

## A note on family coaches

Family members **cannot be hired as coaches** through the Texas Sports Academy Online program. State rules require that coaches be unrelated, third-party vendors operating through an approved partner facility. If a family member is a certified coach or trainer, they would need to be associated with an independent, approved partner facility to work with your student through the program.
