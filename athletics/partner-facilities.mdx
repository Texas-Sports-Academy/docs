Texas Sports Academy Online partners with training facilities across Texas so that students can train close to home in the sport or activity of their choice — all covered by tuition. The network includes rec centers, YMCAs, fitness centers, gyms, sports complexes, and specialty training facilities, and it is actively growing to reach every corner of the state.

<Tip>
  Browse the full list of current partner facilities and submit a request to add one in your area at [sportsacademy.school/facilities](https://sportsacademy.school/facilities).
</Tip>

## What kinds of facilities are in the network

Partner facilities are not limited to elite or specialized sports academies. The network includes:

- **Recreation centers and YMCAs** — community-based facilities with multi-sport programming
- **Fitness centers and gyms** — strength, conditioning, and general athletic training
- **Sports complexes** — multi-field or multi-court facilities for team sports
- **Specialty training facilities** — martial arts studios, dance studios, swim clubs, golf academies, tennis clubs, and more

If a facility type that fits your student's needs is not yet on the list, you can request it (see below).

## How facility coverage works

Sports training at a partner facility is **included in your tuition** — it is part of the school experience, not a separate add-on or a cash benefit.

- **One facility at a time.** Tuition covers training at **one partner facility per student**, on a monthly basis. Your student is set up at a single facility for the month.
- **Switch month to month.** You are never locked in for the year — move to a different partner facility the following month. Exploring options? Switching monthly lets your student sample several facilities before settling in.
- **Multiple sports.** If your student wants to train in more than one sport, choose a **multi-sport facility** so everything is covered under one roof.
- **A second facility at the same time** would be at your own cost — tuition covers one facility per student per month.
- **What's covered varies by facility.** The exact arrangement depends on the facility agreement; at a minimum the program aims to cover at least **one personal session or two to three group sessions per week**.

Each child in a family can train at a **different** facility. And because academics take only about two hours a day, students gain **4\+ extra hours per day** for training compared to peers in traditional school.

### Keep your current team

Training at a partner facility is **individual skill development** — students are **not** placed on facility-specific teams. Your child can keep playing on their current club, travel, or league team; nothing about those commitments changes.

## Managing your student's facility assignment

Enrolled families handle all facility logistics through the training section of the student portal.

<Card title="Manage Training (Enrolled Students)" icon="gear" href="https://sportsacademy.school/home/training">
  View your student's current facility and coach, switch to a different facility, submit a facility request, or ask the team for help finding one near you.
</Card>

From that page you can:

| Action | How |
| --- | --- |
| See current facility and coach | Displayed on the training home screen |
| Switch to a different facility | Select from the dropdown of approved partners |
| Request a new facility | Click **Request a facility** |
| Get help finding one | Click **Help me find one** for team assistance |

<Tip>
  **Your preferred gym isn't approved yet?** You don't have to wait. The team can place your student at any nearby approved facility now (adjusting the sport on file if needed), and switch back to your gym once it joins the network. Apply and get set up rather than waiting on a single facility.
</Tip>

## Requesting a facility that is not yet in the network

If your preferred training location is not listed as a partner facility, you can submit a request from the [facilities page](https://sportsacademy.school/facilities). Your current coach can become a partner too, as long as they work out of a facility that can meet the requirements. Here is what happens after you submit:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Texas Sports Academy reaches out">
    The team contacts the facility on your behalf to introduce the partnership model.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Qualification review">
    The facility must meet all requirements: background checks on coaches, a structured training plan, and the ability to invoice through the program. A coach **cannot be an immediate family member or guardian** of the student.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approval decision">
    Not every request results in approval — facilities must pass the qualification process. You will be notified of the outcome.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Facility joins the network">
    Once approved, the facility appears in the partner directory and your student can be assigned there.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Submitting a request is the primary way the network expands, so do not hesitate to request a facility even if you are unsure it will qualify.

<Note>
  **Are you a coach or facility owner?** Email [partnerships@texassportsacademy.com](mailto:partnerships@texassportsacademy.com) to start the partnership process, or submit your facility on the [facilities page](https://sportsacademy.school/facilities). Partner criteria: an established facility, background checks on staff, a structured training plan, and the ability to invoice.
</Note>

## If a facility does not know about the partnership

Occasionally a facility listed on the partner page may be unaware of the partnership arrangement — staff turnover or onboarding gaps can cause this. If a facility tells you they have not heard of Texas Sports Academy Online, let the team know so they can follow up and make sure the relationship is properly established.
