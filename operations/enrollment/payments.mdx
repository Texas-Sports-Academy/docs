## General

To be eligible to start, a family needs confirmed payment. There are two ways a family pays, and they confirm differently.

### Voucher (TEFA) families

Voucher families confirm through Odyssey, an external state portal that holds their voucher information. The steps are:

1. The voucher information lives in Odyssey.
2. The parents select Texas Sports Academy as their school.
3. A tuition is created for them.
4. They approve the tuition, which sets their Odyssey status to something like *confirmed* — signalling that everything on the Odyssey side is ready.

That Odyssey data then has to make it into our system. Palmer runs a backfill script (informally *Backfill Odyssey Selected*) that takes the latest Odyssey export, matches each row to the family and students we already have, and updates our database so it reflects the voucher portal. For a voucher family, this confirmation **is** their payment being confirmed — there's no separate charge on our side.

### Self-pay families

Self-pay families pay through our portal. Paying records a payment type (their plan) and a paid date, and that's their confirmation. If financial aid, discounts, or a voucher fully cover a self-pay family's tuition, they confirm a $0 balance in the portal instead of paying.

Confirmed payment matters because it controls operational readiness — it's what lets a student count toward a start date's capacity and move toward rostering.

## Developer notes

### What counts as committed

The single source of truth is `hasRealCommitment` (`server/src/lib/enrollment/commitment.ts`): a student has real commitment if they are **odyssey-confirmed**, or have a **paid date with a non-`covered` plan type**.

- **Voucher families** are gated on `odyssey_status = 'confirmed'`. The Odyssey backfill sets that status — it does **not** set `paid_at`. For voucher families, `paid_at` is not the gate.
- **Self-pay families** are gated on `paid_at` plus a real plan type (`annual`, `quarterly`, `monthly`).
- The `$0` **covered** case sets a `covered` plan type, which is **deliberately excluded** from commitment — a covered `paid_at` does not count on its own.

### Self-pay payment paths

- **Real balance:** the checkout session is created by `home.createEnrollmentCheckout`, and the Stripe webhook (`server/src/webhooks/stripe.ts`) records the paid date and plan type when checkout (or a subscription invoice) succeeds.
- **`$0` covered:** `home.confirmZeroBalance`, invoked from the self-pay checkout page, verifies the student's net balance (tuition minus discounts, financial aid, and voucher) is zero, then marks them with a `covered` plan type and parks them in `paid_not_enrolled`. This is part of the self-pay checkout flow — it is **not** the TEFA path, and covered students still rely on Odyssey confirmation to count as committed.

Both paths emit the enrollment-payment event that triggers `auto-enroll`; the daily promote cron is the backstop. See [Readiness and rostering](/operations/enrollment/readiness-and-rostering).

### Odyssey sync mechanics

Two scripts, run operationally by Palmer (not scheduled jobs):

- A **read-only matcher** compares the Odyssey export against our records and flags inconsistencies — mismatched phone numbers, name variants, date-of-birth conflicts, multiple candidates, and so on — so they can be resolved before the backfill writes anything.
- The **backfill** then updates our records from the export. It currently writes the **family-level** `odyssey_status` (a family is `confirmed` only when all its students are); writing the student-level status is a known TODO. When sheet spelling or phone numbers don't match cleanly, the backfill can create a duplicate `lead` student row alongside the real one — a recurring consistency issue (see [Data model notes](/operations/enrollment/data-model-notes)).

`odyssey_status` is a free-text field with these values: `confirmed`, `awaiting_confirmation`, `tuition_needed`, `verification_pending`, or none (absent from the latest export). The Odyssey "Student ID" is an external UUID, distinct from our internal student id. The backfill never marks a student `paid_at`-paid or `enrolled` — but for voucher families, the Odyssey status it writes **is** the payment-confirmation signal.

<Note>
  Keep the terms `paid_not_enrolled`, `enrolled`, `odyssey_status`, and `covered` precise. They are not interchangeable.
</Note>
